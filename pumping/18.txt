<h1 class="heading__title">XML Sitemap</h1>
<div class="heading__params">
<span aria-hidden="true" class="heading__label">
										Last updated:
									</span>
<span aria-label="The date of last update" class="heading__caption">
										February  7, 2022
									</span>
</div>
</div>
<div class="post-detail__text typo">
<section aria-label="XML Sitemap in short" class="highlighted-box highlighted-box--with-background typo-block"><div aria-hidden="true" class="highlighted-box__title">
<span class="highlighted-box__title-icon"><i class="post-type-icon post-type-icon--reversed-style" style="background-image: url(/assets/images/icons/post-types/xml-sitemap--reversed.svg)"></i></span>XML Sitemap in short
	</div>
<div class="highlighted-box__content">
<p>An XML Sitemap is a special document which lists all pages on a website to provide search engines with an overview of all the available content.</p>
<p>It's strongly recommended to implement an XML Sitemap, especially on larger websites (500+ pages).</p>
<p>Stick to the following best practices when implementing an XML Sitemap:</p>
<ul>
<li>Keep the XML Sitemap up to date with your website's content.</li>
<li>Make sure it's clean: only indexable pages should be included.</li>
<li>Reference the XML Sitemap from your robots.txt file.</li>
<li>Don't list more than 50.000 URLs in a single XML Sitemap.</li>
<li>Make sure the (uncompressed) file size doesn't exceed 50MB.</li>
<li>Don't obsess about the lastmod, priority and changefreq properties.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</section>

<h2 id="what-is-an-xml-sitemap">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#what-is-an-xml-sitemap" tabindex="-1"></a>What is an XML Sitemap?</h2>
<p>An XML Sitemap is a special document which lists all pages on a website and is meant for search engines. Compare it to a telephone book: it tells the search engine what content is available and how to reach it. Furthermore some extra information can be provided, such as when the content was last updated and what the relative importance is of the content.</p>
<p>XML Sitemaps are very useful for search engines, as it provides them with a single overview of all the available content at once. This serves for them as both a starting point for the first time they go through your website as a way to quickly discover newly added content.</p>
<p>What's important to note is the distinction between XML sitemaps and "regular" sitemaps (also called "HTML sitemaps"). Those sitemaps are meant for your <strong>visitors</strong> to find content on your website, while XML sitemaps are meant for <strong>search engines</strong>.</p>
<h2 id="why-should-you-care-about-xml-sitemap">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#why-should-you-care-about-xml-sitemap" tabindex="-1"></a>Why should you care about XML Sitemap?</h2>
<p>XML Sitemaps help search engines to assess your website's content, and is a mechanism to notify them of new or updated content. Therefore it's recommended to implement them whenever feasible. And especially for larger websites (500+ pages) they become a real must-have.</p>
<h2 id="what-does-an-xml-sitemap-look-like">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#what-does-an-xml-sitemap-look-like" tabindex="-1"></a>What does an XML Sitemap look like?</h2>
<p>An XML Sitemap is meant for search engines, and thus they are formatted in a language that's easy to understand for computers: XML. Fortunately XML is also quite readable for humans as well, so let's take a look at an example:</p>
<section aria-label="A fragment of code" class="code-box"><div class="code-box__code">
<figure class="highlight"><pre><code class="language-text" data-lang="text">&lt;?xml version="1.0" encoding="UTF-8"?&gt;
&lt;urlset xmlns="http://www.sitemaps.org/schemas/sitemap/0.9"&gt;
	&lt;url&gt;
		&lt;loc&gt;https://www.contentkingapp.com/&lt;/loc&gt;
		&lt;lastmod&gt;2017-06-14T19:55:25+02:00&lt;/lastmod&gt;
	&lt;/url&gt;
	&lt;url&gt;
		&lt;loc&gt;https://www.contentkingapp.com/blog/&lt;/loc&gt;
		&lt;lastmod&gt;2016-06-24T10:23:20+02:00&lt;/lastmod&gt;
	&lt;/url&gt;
&lt;/urlset&gt;</code></pre></figure>
</div></section>
<p>Now, to understand what's going on let's dissect the individual parts!</p>

<h3 id="xml-header">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#xml-header" tabindex="-1"></a>XML Header</h3>
<p><code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">&lt;?xml version="1.0" encoding="UTF-8"?&gt;</code></p>
<p>This header denotes that the contents is structured according to version 1.0 of the XML standard and describe the character encoding. It basically informs search engines what they can expect from the file.</p>
<h3 id="definition-of-the-url-set">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#definition-of-the-url-set" tabindex="-1"></a>Definition of the URL set</h3>
<p><code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">&lt;urlset xmlns="http://www.sitemaps.org/schemas/sitemap/0.9"&gt;</code></p>
<p>This <em>urlset</em> definition encapsulates all the URLs contained in the sitemap and describes which version of the XML Sitemap standard is used. Note that the <em>urlset</em> gets closed at the bottom of the document:</p>
<p><code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">&lt;/urlset&gt;</code></p>
<h3 id="definition-of-the-individual-urls">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#definition-of-the-individual-urls" tabindex="-1"></a>Definition of the individual URLs</h3>
<section aria-label="A fragment of code" class="code-box"><div class="code-box__code">
<figure class="highlight"><pre><code class="language-text" data-lang="text">&lt;url&gt;
	&lt;loc&gt;https://www.contentkingapp.com/&lt;/loc&gt;
	&lt;lastmod&gt;2017-06-14T19:55:25+02:00&lt;/lastmod&gt;
&lt;/url&gt;</code></pre></figure>
</div></section>
<p>Finally we get to the most important part: the definition of the individuals URLs through the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">url</code>-tag. Every URL definition needs to contain at least the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">loc</code>-tag (short for <em>location</em>). The value of this tag should be the full URL of the page, including the protocol (e.g. <em>"http://"</em>).</p>
<p>On top of that every URL definition may contain the following optional properties:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">lastmod</code>: the date of when the content on that URL was last modified. The date is in "<a href="https://www.w3.org/TR/NOTE-datetime" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="extlink">W3C datetime</span><span class="show-for-sr"> (opens in a new tab)</span></a>" format.</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">priority</code>: the priority of the URL, relative to your own website on a scale between 0.0 and 1.0.</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">changefreq</code>: how often the content on the URL is expected to change. Possible values are always, hourly, daily, weekly, monthly, yearly and never.</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="where-should-i-place-my-xml-sitemap">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#where-should-i-place-my-xml-sitemap" tabindex="-1"></a>Where should I place my XML Sitemap?</h2>
<p>Just like your website's pages, the XML Sitemap resides on its own URL. Usually the URL for an XML Sitemap is /<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">sitemap.xml</code>, and it's recommended to follow this convention to make it easy for search engines to discover it.</p>
<p>However, if for any reason this is not possible you can choose a different location or filename, as long as you reference it in your <a href="/academy/robotstxt/">robots.txt file</a> through the Sitemap-directive:</p>
<section aria-label="A fragment of code" class="code-box"><div class="code-box__code">
<figure class="highlight"><pre><code class="language-text" data-lang="text">Sitemap: http://www.example.com/alternativelocation/alternativefilename.xml</code></pre></figure>
</div></section>
<h2 id="are-there-any-limitations-for-xml-sitemaps">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#are-there-any-limitations-for-xml-sitemaps" tabindex="-1"></a>Are there any limitations for XML Sitemaps?</h2>
<p>XML Sitemaps have a couple of limitations to keep in mind:</p>
<ul>
<li>They must not contain more than maximum 50.000 URLs.</li>
<li>Their file size is limited to 50MB when uncompressed.</li>
</ul>
<p>If your XML Sitemap exceeds these limits you need to split them across multiple XML Sitemaps and use an XML Sitemap Index.</p>
<h2 id="what-is-an-xml-sitemap-index">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#what-is-an-xml-sitemap-index" tabindex="-1"></a>What is an XML Sitemap Index?</h2>
<p>Whenever you cross the limitations for a single XML Sitemap you need to split them up into separate XML Sitemaps and bundle them together with an XML Sitemap Index. This index is a separate XML-file which references the various XML Sitemaps.</p>
<p>Let's take a look at an example:</p>
<section aria-label="A fragment of code" class="code-box"><div class="code-box__code">
<figure class="highlight"><pre><code class="language-text" data-lang="text">&lt;?xml version="1.0" encoding="UTF-8"?&gt;
&lt;sitemapindex xmlns="http://www.sitemaps.org/schemas/sitemap/0.9"&gt;
	&lt;sitemap&gt;
		&lt;loc&gt;http://www.example.com/sitemap1.xml.gz&lt;/loc&gt;
		&lt;lastmod&gt;2004-10-01T18:23:17+00:00&lt;/lastmod&gt;
	&lt;/sitemap&gt;
	&lt;sitemap&gt;
		&lt;loc&gt;http://www.example.com/sitemap2.xml.gz&lt;/loc&gt;
		&lt;lastmod&gt;2005-01-01&lt;/lastmod&gt;
	&lt;/sitemap&gt;
&lt;/sitemapindex&gt;</code></pre></figure>
</div></section>
<p>This XML Sitemap Index references two XML Sitemaps: <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">sitemap1.xml.gz</code> and <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">sitemap2.xml.gz</code>. Let's dissect this file as well!</p>
<h3 id="xml-header">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#xml-header" tabindex="-1"></a>XML Header</h3>
<p><code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">&lt;?xml version="1.0" encoding="UTF-8"?&gt;</code></p>
<p>Nothing new here, just like with the XML Sitemap file we first define that the file is in XML format and which character encoding is used.</p>
<h3 id="definition-of-the-sitemap-index">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#definition-of-the-sitemap-index" tabindex="-1"></a>Definition of the Sitemap Index</h3>
<p><code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">&lt;sitemapindex xmlns="http://www.sitemaps.org/schemas/sitemap/0.9"&gt;</code></p>
<p>Now, instead of a <em>urlset</em> definition we see a <em>sitemapindex</em> definition. This definition encapsulates all the sitemaps contained in the sitemap index and again which version of the XML Sitemap standard is used. Just like the <em>urlset</em> definition the <em>sitemapindex</em> definition is closed at the bottom of the document:</p>
<p><code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">&lt;/sitemapindex&gt;</code></p>
<h3 id="definition-of-the-individual-sitemaps">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#definition-of-the-individual-sitemaps" tabindex="-1"></a>Definition of the individual sitemaps</h3>
<section aria-label="A fragment of code" class="code-box"><div class="code-box__code">
<figure class="highlight"><pre><code class="language-text" data-lang="text">&lt;sitemap&gt;
	&lt;loc&gt;http://www.example.com/sitemap1.xml.gz&lt;/loc&gt;
	&lt;lastmod&gt;2004-10-01T18:23:17+00:00&lt;/lastmod&gt;
&lt;/sitemap&gt;</code></pre></figure>
</div></section>
<p>And then on to the meat: the actual definition of the individuals sitemaps. Just like for URLs, every sitemap definition needs to contain at least the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">loc</code>-tag, containing the full URL of the individual XML Sitemap.</p>
<p>On top of that the sitemap definition may optionally contain a lastmod definition. The date when the referenced XML sitemap was last updated. Again in "<a href="https://www.w3.org/TR/NOTE-datetime" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="extlink">W3C datetime</span><span class="show-for-sr"> (opens in a new tab)</span></a>" format.</p>
<h2 id="where-should-i-place-my-xml-sitemap-index">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#where-should-i-place-my-xml-sitemap-index" tabindex="-1"></a>Where should I place my XML Sitemap Index?</h2>
<p>Similar to XML Sitemaps there is a convention for the location and filename of the XML Sitemap Index: /<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">sitemap_index.xml</code>. But again you're free to deviate from this, as long as you reference it in your robots.txt file:</p>
<section aria-label="A fragment of code" class="code-box"><div class="code-box__code">
<figure class="highlight"><pre><code class="language-text" data-lang="text">Sitemap: http://www.example.com/alternativelocation/alternativefilename.xml</code></pre></figure>
</div></section>

<h2 id="best-practices-for-xml-sitemap">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#best-practices-for-xml-sitemap" tabindex="-1"></a>Best practices for XML Sitemap</h2>
<p>When implementing XML Sitemaps it's essential to follow these best practices.</p>
<h3 id="keep-your-xml-sitemap-up-to-date">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#keep-your-xml-sitemap-up-to-date" tabindex="-1"></a>Keep your XML Sitemap up-to-date</h3>
<p>Make sure that your XML Sitemap provides an up-to-date picture of your website. Whenever a page is removed it should also be delisted from your XML Sitemap. If you're using the optional <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">lastmod</code>-tag, make sure to update the timestamp whenever the page changes.</p>
<section aria-label="Pro tip" class="highlighted-box typo-block highlighted-box--with-icon highlighted-box--protip-style"><div aria-hidden="true" class="highlighted-box__title">Pro tip</div>
<div class="highlighted-box__content">
<p>The <a href="/academy/index-coverage/faq/indexed-not-submitted/">Indexed, not submitted in sitemap</a> issue in Google Search Console's Index Coverage Report is very useful to verify whether your XML sitemap includes all of your indexable pages.</p>
</div></section>
<h3 id="only-include-indexable-pages-in-your-xml-sitemap">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#only-include-indexable-pages-in-your-xml-sitemap" tabindex="-1"></a>Only include indexable pages in your XML Sitemap</h3>
<p>Your XML Sitemap should only describe indexable pages. This means that you should leave out all URLs pointing to <a href="/academy/redirects/">redirects</a> (e.g. 301 status code) and missing pages (e.g. <a href="/academy/http-status-codes/#404">404 status code</a>).</p>
<p>Furthermore these pages need to be indexable, which means they are accessible for search engines (no exclusion in <a href="/academy/robotstxt/">robots.txt</a>) and there are no directives telling search engines not to index the page (such as <a href="/academy/meta-robots-tag/">meta robots</a>, <a href="/academy/canonical/">canonical links</a> or x-robots-tag).</p>
<section aria-label="Frequently Asked Questions" class="highlighted-box typo-block highlighted-box--with-background highlighted-box--with-icon highlighted-box--faq-style"><div aria-hidden="true" class="highlighted-box__title">Frequently Asked Questions</div>
<div class="highlighted-box__content">
<p>Learn what do these issues mean in GSC's Index Coverage report:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/academy/index-coverage/faq/submitted-noindex/">Submitted URL marked ‘noindex’</a></li>
<li><a href="/academy/index-coverage/faq/submitted-soft-404/">Submitted URL seems to be a Soft 404</a></li>
<li><a href="/academy/index-coverage/faq/submitted-crawl-issue/">Submitted URL has crawl issue</a></li>
</ul>
</div></section>
<h3 id="stick-to-the-default-location-and-filename">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#stick-to-the-default-location-and-filename" tabindex="-1"></a>Stick to the default location and filename</h3>
<p>Whenever possible stick to the default location and filename for your XML Sitemap (<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/sitemap.xml</code>) and XML Sitemap Index (<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/sitemap_index.xml</code>). This makes it the easiest for search engines to find them.</p>
<h3 id="reference-the-xml-sitemap-in-your-robots-txt-file">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#reference-the-xml-sitemap-in-your-robots-txt-file" tabindex="-1"></a>Reference the XML Sitemap in your robots.txt file</h3>
<p>When you're deviating from the convention for the URL of your XML Sitemap or XML Sitemap Index you should reference it in your robots.txt file. However, even if you're sticking to the standard URL it's recommended to include a reference to it in your robots.txt to ensure discoverability by search engines.</p>
<h3 id="dont-obsess-about-lastmod-priority-and-changefreq">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#dont-obsess-about-lastmod-priority-and-changefreq" tabindex="-1"></a>Don't obsess about lastmod, priority and changefreq</h3>
<p>Although for every URL you can define the lastmod, priority and changefreq properties, this is fully optional. Defining them won't hurt, and there may be a slight chance search engines will use this information, but it's generally understood that <a href="https://www.seroundtable.com/google-sitemap-priority-field-a-bag-of-noise-23645.html" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="extlink">search engines don't pay (much) attention to them</span><span class="show-for-sr"> (opens in a new tab)</span></a>.</p>
<h3 id="stick-to-the-limits-for-xml-sitemaps">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#stick-to-the-limits-for-xml-sitemaps" tabindex="-1"></a>Stick to the limits for XML Sitemaps</h3>
<p>Make sure that your XML Sitemaps don't contain more than 50.000 URLs and the uncompressed filesize is limited to 50MB. Whenever you exceed either limit you should split the XML Sitemap up and use an XML Sitemap Index.</p>
<h2 id="faq">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#faq" tabindex="-1"></a>Frequently asked questions about XML Sitemap</h2>
<ol>
<li><a href="/academy/xml-sitemap/#what-does-the-gz-extention-mean">What does the .gz extension mean?</a></li>
</ol>
<h3 id="what-does-the-gz-extention-mean">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#what-does-the-gz-extention-mean" tabindex="-1"></a>1. What does the .gz extension mean?</h3>
<p>The .gz extension is added to the filename when the XML Sitemap is compressed (via gzip compression). XML Sitemaps containing many URLs usually grow to significant file sizes, and through the use of compression the impact of this on disk storage and network transfer time can be reduced.</p>

<h2 id="what-are-cookies">What are cookies?</h2>
<p>Cookies are small text files which are stored on the browser or hard drive of your Device when you visit a webpage or use an application. Cookies may be needed to show the webpage or application on your Device and are also used to enhance the user experience. Cookies cannot damage your Devices or the files saved on it. There are different types of cookies. Some cookies come directly from our website (first party cookies) and others come from third parties which place cookies on our site (third party cookies).</p>
<p>Cookies can be stored for varying lengths of time on your browser or device. Session cookies are deleted from your computer or device when you close your web-browser. Persistent cookies will remain stored on your computer or Device until deleted or until they reach their expiry date.</p>
<h2 id="how-do-we-use-cookies">How do we use cookies?</h2>
<h3 id="essential-technical-cookies">Essential (Technical) cookies</h3>
<p>We try to provide an advanced and user-friendly website that adapts automatically to needs and wishes of our visitors and users. To achieve this, we use technical cookies to i.e. show you our Website, to make it function correctly, to create your ContentKing account, to sign you in and to manage your requests. These technical cookies are necessary for our website to function properly.</p>
<h3 id="functional-cookies">Functional cookies</h3>
<p>We also use functional cookies to remember your preferences and settings (as username, password, language etc.) and to help you to use the Website efficiently and effectively. These functional cookies are not strictly necessary for the functioning of our Website, but they add functionality for you and enhance your experience.</p>
<h3 id="analytical-cookies">Analytical cookies</h3>
<p>We use analytical cookies in order to collect statistics about the use and visits of the Website. These analytic cookies generate statistical and other information about website use by means of cookies, which are stored on users’ Devices. The information generated relating to our Website is used to create reports about the use of the Website. Analytical cookies may be placed without your consent, unless such cookies have an impact on your privacy. In such cases, prior consent will be asked for.</p>
<h3 id="google-analytics">Google Analytics</h3>
<p>We use Google Analytics cookies in order to collect statistics about the use and visits of the Website. With these analytical cookies a permanent cookie is saved on your device to register your use of the Website. Google Analytics analyses this data and provides us with the results. This way, we obtain insight into the traffic of the Website and the way in which the Website is used. Based on this information we are able to make specific adjustments to the Website.</p>
<p>The information that we obtain is transferred to Google and stored by Google on servers outside the European Economic Area. We have entered into a data processing agreement with Google in which agreements have been made regarding the processing of personal data.</p>
<p>If you would like more information about analytical cookies and their expiry date, please visit the Google Analytics <a href="https://developers.google.com/analytics/devguides/collection/gajs/cookie-usage">information page</a>. Google also offers an <a href="https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout?hl=en">opt-out option</a> for data collection within the scope of Google Analytics.</p>
<h3 id="marketing-cookies">Marketing cookies</h3>
<p>We use third party marketing cookies in order to follow your internet browsing behavior and collect data and information on your browsing behavior from various websites you visited. This information is used to make the content of the displayed advertisements as relevant as possible, and to limit repetition of the same advertisements.</p>
<h3 id="social-plug-ins">Social plug-ins</h3>
<p>By using the Website you can get access to social media websites, such as Facebook, Twitter and LinkedIn. These buttons come with a code which has been made by these networks themselves. Using these so called social plug-ins you can login to your social media profile and subsequently share information from the Website with others. With these social plug-ins third party marketing cookies are saved on your Devices. These cookies serve the purpose of optimizing your user experience.</p>
<p>Companies such as LinkedIn, Twitter and Facebook may share your personal data outside the European Economic Area. Please read our Privacy Policy <a href="/legal/privacy-policy/">here</a> and the respective social network’s privacy statement to see how these companies treat your (personal) data.</p>
<h3 id="pixels">Pixels</h3>
<p>We also place pixels of third parties. A pixel keeps track of your surfing behavior and how you use the Website. These data are aggregated and give us information about the target group that visits our website. Based on this information, we can show an advertisement to a specific or similar target group on the website of the third party that produced the pixel.</p>
<h2 id="disable-or-delete-cookies">Disable or delete cookies</h2>
<p>When you first visit the Website, we ask you to give consent for cookies that are not necessary or functional and that have an impact on your privacy. If you do not give consent, these cookies will not be placed. In that case, you may not be able to use all the functionalities on our Website.</p>
<p>You can configure your browser so that you do not receive any cookies the next time you use the website. You can read here how you can remove different types of cookies in different browsers. Please consult the help-function of your browser if your browser is not listed below.</p>
