<h1 class="heading__title">Find and Fix Index Coverage Errors in Google Search Console</h1>
<div class="heading__params">
<span aria-hidden="true" class="heading__label">
										Last updated:
									</span>

<div class="post-detail__text typo">
<section aria-label="Google Search Console Index Coverage in short" class="highlighted-box highlighted-box--with-background typo-block"><div aria-hidden="true" class="highlighted-box__title">
<span class="highlighted-box__title-icon"><i class="post-type-icon post-type-icon--reversed-style" style="background-image: url(/assets/images/icons/post-types/gsc-coverage-report--reversed.svg)"></i></span>Google Search Console Index Coverage in short
	</div>
<div class="highlighted-box__content">
<p>Google Search Console's Index Coverage report provides feedback on your site‚Äôs crawling and indexing process.</p>
<p>The reported issues break down in four statuses:</p>
<ol>
<li>Valid</li>
<li>Valid with warnings</li>
<li>Error</li>
<li>Excluded</li>
</ol>
<p>Each status consists of different issue types that zoom in on specific issues Google has found on your site.</p>
</div>
</section>

<p>As you know, Google Search Console is an essential part of every SEO‚Äôs toolbox.</p>
<p>Among other things, Google Search Console reports on your organic performance and how they fared when <a href="/academy/control-crawl-indexing/">crawling and indexing</a> your site. The latter topic is covered in their 'Index Coverage report', which this article is all about.</p>
<p>After reading this article, you‚Äôll have a firm understanding of how to leverage the Index Coverage report to improve your SEO performance.</p>
<div class="media-container" style="max-width: 600px;"><picture><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-coverage-report-discovered-crawled-indexed@1x.avif" type="image/avif"/><source srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-coverage-report-discovered-crawled-indexed@1x.avif, /media/content/contentking-gsc-coverage-report-discovered-crawled-indexed@2x.avif 2x" type="image/avif"/><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-coverage-report-discovered-crawled-indexed@1x.webp" type="image/webp"/><source srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-coverage-report-discovered-crawled-indexed@1x.webp, /media/content/contentking-gsc-coverage-report-discovered-crawled-indexed@2x.webp 2x" type="image/webp"/><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-coverage-report-discovered-crawled-indexed@1x.png"/><img alt="Discovered, Crawled and Indexed states" class="image" height="175" src="/media/content/contentking-gsc-coverage-report-discovered-crawled-indexed@1x.png" srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-coverage-report-discovered-crawled-indexed@1x.png, /media/content/contentking-gsc-coverage-report-discovered-crawled-indexed@2x.png 2x" title="Discovered, Crawled and Indexed states" width="600"/></picture></div>
<p>Before we dig in, here‚Äôs a brief primer on discovering, crawling, indexing, and ranking:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Discovering:</strong> in order to crawl a URL, search engines first need to discover it. There are various ways they can do so, such as: following links from other pages (both on-site and off-site) and processing XML sitemaps. URLs that have been discovered are then queued for crawling.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Crawling</strong>: during the crawling phase, search engines request URLs and gather information from them. After an URL is received, it‚Äôs handed over to the Indexer, which handles the <em>Indexing</em> process.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Indexing:</strong> during indexing, search engines try to make sense of the information produced by the crawling phase. To put it somewhat simply, during indexing a URL‚Äôs authority and relevance for queries is determined.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>When URLs are indexed, they can appear in search engine result pages (SERPs).</p>
<p>Let this all sink in for a minute.</p>
<p>This means your pages can only appear in the SERPs if your pages made it successfully through the last phase ‚Äî indexing.</p>

<h2 id="what-is">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#what-is" tabindex="-1"></a>What is the Google Search Console Index Coverage report?</h2>
<p>When Google is crawling and indexing your site, they keep track of the results and report them in <a href="https://support.google.com/webmasters/answer/7440203?hl=en" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="extlink">Google Search Console‚Äôs Index Coverage report</span><span class="show-for-sr"> (opens in a new tab)</span></a>.</p>
<p>It‚Äôs basically feedback on the more technical details of your site‚Äôs crawling and indexing process. In case they detect pressing issue, they <a href="/academy/index-coverage/faq/new-index-coverage-issue/">send notifications</a>. These notifications are usually delayed though, so dont solely rely on these notifcations to learn about high-impact SEO issues.</p>
<p>Google's feedback is categorized in four statuses:</p>
<ol>
<li>Valid</li>
<li>Valid with warnings</li>
<li>Excluded</li>
<li>Error</li>
</ol>

<h2 id="when-use">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#when-use" tabindex="-1"></a>When should you use the Index Coverage report?</h2>
<p><a href="https://support.google.com/webmasters/answer/7440203#landing-page-what-look-for" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="extlink">Google says</span><span class="show-for-sr"> (opens in a new tab)</span></a> that if your site has fewer than 500 pages, you probably don‚Äôt need to use the Index Coverage report. For sites like this, they recommend using their <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">site:</code> operator.</p>
<p><strong>We strongly disagree with this.</strong></p>
<p>If organic traffic from Google is essential to your business, you <strong>do</strong> need to use their Index Coverage report, because it provides detailed information and is much more reliable than using their <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">site:</code> operator to debug indexing issues.</p>
<h2 id="explained">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#explained" tabindex="-1"></a>The Index Coverage report explained</h2>
<div class="media-container" style="max-width: 600px;"><picture><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-coverage-report-look-like@1x.avif" type="image/avif"/><source srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-coverage-report-look-like@1x.avif, /media/content/contentking-gsc-coverage-report-look-like@2x.avif 2x" type="image/avif"/><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-coverage-report-look-like@1x.webp" type="image/webp"/><source srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-coverage-report-look-like@1x.webp, /media/content/contentking-gsc-coverage-report-look-like@2x.webp 2x" type="image/webp"/><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-coverage-report-look-like@1x.png"/><img alt="Screenshot of Google Search Console‚Äôs Index Coverage report including details" class="image" height="467" loading="lazy" src="/media/content/contentking-gsc-coverage-report-look-like@1x.png" srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-coverage-report-look-like@1x.png, /media/content/contentking-gsc-coverage-report-look-like@2x.png 2x" title="Screenshot of Google Search Console‚Äôs Index Coverage report including details" width="600"/></picture></div>
<p>The screenshot above is from a fairly large site with lots of interesting technical challenges.</p>
<p>Find your own Index Coverage report by following these steps:</p>
<section class="highlighted-box typo-block highlighted-box--with-icon highlighted-box--steps-style"><div class="highlighted-box__content">
<ol>
<li>Log on to Google Search Console.</li>
<li>Choose a property.</li>
<li>Click <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Coverage</code> under <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Index</code> in the left navigation.</li>
</ol>
</div></section>
<p>The Index Coverage report distinguishes among four status categories:</p>
<ol>
<li>
<a href="/academy/index-coverage/#valid">Valid</a>: pages that have been indexed.</li>
<li>
<a href="/academy/index-coverage/#valid-with-warnings">Valid with warnings</a>: pages that have been indexed, but which contain some issues you may want to look at.</li>
<li>
<a href="/academy/index-coverage/#excluded">Excluded</a>: pages that weren‚Äôt indexed because search engines picked up clear signals they shouldn‚Äôt index them.</li>
<li>
<a href="/academy/index-coverage/#error">Error</a>: pages that couldn‚Äôt be indexed for some reason.</li>
</ol>

<h4 id="duplicate-submitted-url-not-canonical">Duplicate, submitted URL not selected as canonical</h4>
<p>You‚Äôve submitted these URLs through an XML sitemap, but they don‚Äôt have a canonical URL set. Google considers these URLs duplicates of other URLs, and has therefore chosen to canonicalize these URLs with Google-selected canonical URLs.</p>

<p><strong>Action required:</strong> add proper canonical URLs that point to the preferred version of the URL.</p>

<h4 id="excluded-noindex">Excluded by ‚Äònoindex‚Äô tag</h4>
<p>These URLs haven‚Äôt been indexed by Google because of <a href="/academy/meta-robots-tag/#noindex">the noindex directive</a> (either in the HTML source or HTTP header).</p>
<p><strong>Action required:</strong> make sure there aren‚Äôt important URLs among the ones listed in this overview. If you do find important URLs, remove the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">noindex directive</code>, and use the URL Inspection tool to request indexing. <h3 id="-what-information-does-the-index-coverage-report-contain">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#-what-information-does-the-index-coverage-report-contain" tabindex="-1"></a>üí° What information does the Index Coverage report contain?</h3>
<p>The Index Coverage report provides feedback from Google on how they fared when crawling and indexing your website. It contains valuable information that helps you improve your SEO performance.</p>
<h3 id="-when-should-you-use-the-index-coverage-report">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#-when-should-you-use-the-index-coverage-report" tabindex="-1"></a>ü§î When should you use the Index Coverage report?</h3>
<p>While Google says that the Index Coverage report is only useful for sites with more than 500 pages, we recommend that anyone who heavily relies on organic traffic uses it. It provides so much detailed information and is much more reliable than using their site: operator to debug indexing issues, you don't want to miss out on this.</p>
<h3 id="-how-often-should-i-check-the-index-coverage-report">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#-how-often-should-i-check-the-index-coverage-report" tabindex="-1"></a>üïí How often should I check the Index Coverage report?</h3>
<p>That depends on what you‚Äôve got going on at your website. If it‚Äôs a simple website with a few hundred pages, you may want to check it once a month. If you‚Äôve got millions of pages and add thousands of pages on a weekly basis, we‚Äôd recommend checking the most important issue types once a week.</p>
<h3 id="-why-are-so-many-of-my-pages-listed-with-the-excluded-status">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#-why-are-so-many-of-my-pages-listed-with-the-excluded-status" tabindex="-1"></a>‚õî Why are so many of my pages listed with the ‚ÄúExcluded‚Äù status?</h3>
<p>There are various reasons for this, but we often see that the majority of these URLs are canonicalized URLs, redirecting URLs and URLs that are blocked through the site‚Äôs robots.txt.</p>
<p>Especially for large sites, that adds up quickly.</p>

<h2 id="what-are-cookies">What are cookies?</h2>
<p>Cookies are small text files which are stored on the browser or hard drive of your Device when you visit a webpage or use an application. There are different types of cookies. Some cookies come directly from our website (first party cookies) and others come from third parties which place cookies on our site (third party cookies).</p>
<p>Cookies can be stored for varying lengths of time on your browser or device. Session cookies are deleted from your computer or device when you close your web-browser. Persistent cookies will remain stored on your computer or Device until deleted or until they reach their expiry date.</p>
