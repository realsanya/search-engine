<h1 class="heading__title">SEO for PDF files: learn how to optimize them</h1>
<div class="heading__params">
<span aria-hidden="true" class="heading__label">
										Last updated:
									</span>
<span aria-label="The date of last update" class="heading__caption">
										October 12, 2021
									</span>
</div>
</div>
<div class="post-detail__text typo">
<section aria-label="PDF SEO in short" class="highlighted-box highlighted-box--with-background typo-block"><div aria-hidden="true" class="highlighted-box__title">
<span class="highlighted-box__title-icon"><i class="post-type-icon post-type-icon--reversed-style" style="background-image: url(/assets/images/icons/post-types/pdf-documents--reversed.svg)"></i></span>PDF SEO in short
	</div>
<div class="highlighted-box__content">
<p>Just like your website's pages, you can optimize PDF files for search engine findability. Keep the following things in mind when doing so:</p>
<ul>
<li>Choose a SEO friendly filename.</li>
<li>Define a good title and description.</li>
<li>Use headings and include relevant outgoing links.</li>
<li>Make sure they don't cause duplicate content issues.</li>
<li>Don't save PDF files as images.</li>
<li>Optimize for mobile and file size.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</section>
<div aria-label="Table of contents" class="highlighted-box" role="navigation">
<div aria-hidden="true" class="highlighted-box__title">Table of contents</div>
<div class="highlighted-box__content">
<ul class="navigation-list">
<li class="navigation-list__item navigation-list__item--primary"><a href="#the-basics-of-optimizing-pdf-files">The basics of optimizing PDF files</a></li>
<li class="navigation-list__item navigation-list__item--primary"><a href="#integrate-pdf-files-in-your-website">Integrate PDF files in your website</a></li>
<li class="navigation-list__item navigation-list__item--primary"><a href="#prevent-duplicate-content-canonicalize-pdf-files">Prevent duplicate content: canonicalize PDF files</a></li>
<li class="navigation-list__item navigation-list__item--primary"><a href="#dont-save-pdf-files-as-images">Don't save PDF files as images</a></li>
<li class="navigation-list__item navigation-list__item--primary"><a href="#optimize-pdf-files-for-mobile">Optimize PDF files for Mobile</a></li>
<li class="navigation-list__item navigation-list__item--primary"><a href="#optimize-pdf-file-size">Optimize PDF file size</a></li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
<p>Google crawls billions of documents a day. Documents can be for instance pages, images or PDF files. Often people forget to optimize their PDF files. That's a missed opportunity as PDF files have - just like regular pages - the potential to generate enormous amounts of traffic.</p>
<p>In this article we'll show you how you can SEO optimize your PDF files. Millions of websites are using PDF files to serve for instance whitepapers, manuals and e-books.</p>
<h2 id="the-basics-of-optimizing-pdf-files">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#the-basics-of-optimizing-pdf-files" tabindex="-1"></a>The basics of optimizing PDF files</h2>
<div class="media-container" style="max-width: 851px;"><img alt="PDF file serp result" class="image" height="260" src="/wp-content/uploads/2017/09/pdf-file-serp-result.png" title="PDF file serp result" width="851"/></div>
<p>Optimizing PDF files is slightly different from optimizing pages, but the same principles apply: perform keyword research and incorporate keywords.</p>
<p>When we're talking about optimizing PDF files, this comes down to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Choose a SEO friendly filename</li>
<li>Define a good title and description</li>
<li>Use headings</li>
<li>Include relevant links</li>
</ul>

<h3 id="choose-a-seo-friendly-filename">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#choose-a-seo-friendly-filename" tabindex="-1"></a>Choose a SEO friendly filename</h3>
<p>The filename of the PDF file will often be shown in the search results. It has to be meaningful for visitors, which often means it includes relevant keywords and it shouldn't be too long.</p>
<p>Say you've written a PDF manual for the 46" Samsung F6320 Series 6 Smart TV, you could make the filename: <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">samsung-f6320-series-6-45-inch</code>. Hyphens are included to indicate spaces, separating the words.</p>
<h3 id="define-a-good-title-and-meta-description">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#define-a-good-title-and-meta-description" tabindex="-1"></a>Define a good title and meta description</h3>
<p>Similar to pages, PDF files have a title and meta description too. Define these to make sure your PDF file is well optimized for both search engines and visitors.</p>
<section aria-label="Defining title and meta description in Acrobat Reader" class="highlighted-box typo-block highlighted-box--with-icon highlighted-box--steps-style"><div aria-hidden="true" class="highlighted-box__title">Defining title and meta description in Acrobat Reader</div>
<div class="highlighted-box__content">
<ol>
<li>Go to <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">File</code>
</li>
<li>Click the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Document Properties</code>
</li>
<li>Fill the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">title</code> in the Title field</li>
<li>Fill the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">meta description</code> element in the Subject field</li>
</ol>
</div></section>
<p>For the Samsung TV you could fill in:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<strong>Title</strong>: "Samsung 46" F6320 Series 6 Smart 3D Full HD LED TV"</li>
<li>
<strong>Subject</strong>: "Looking for Samsung 46" F6320 Series 6 Smart 3D Full HD LED TV? Ordered today, delivered by tomorrow!"</li>
</ul>
<p>Remember, titles and meta descriptions should be unique across your entire website. That means a PDF file's meta description can't be the same as a page's meta description.</p>
<h3 id="use-headings-to-structure-your-pdf-files">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#use-headings-to-structure-your-pdf-files" tabindex="-1"></a>Use headings to structure your PDF files</h3>
<p>Again, similar to pages: <a href="/academy/headings/">use headings to structure your PDF files</a>. They're important for visitors as well as search engines to quickly understand the hierarchy and content of the document.</p>
<p>The same rules apply: you should only use one <a href="/academy/headings/faq/h1/">H1 heading</a> per PDF file and it has to be unique. You can use multiple H2-H6 headings as you see fit, but keep the hierarchy of the PDF file in mind.</p>
<h3 id="include-links">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#include-links" tabindex="-1"></a>Include links</h3>
<p>Search engines treat PDF files similar to pages. This means PDF files carry <a href="/academy/authority/#page-authority">page authority</a> too, and the links included in the PDF files pass <a href="/academy/authority/#link-authority">link authority</a> just like normal links would. Include links to relevant sections in the website, and if it makes sense to link out to external websites feel free to do so too.</p>

<h2 id="integrate-pdf-files-in-your-website">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#integrate-pdf-files-in-your-website" tabindex="-1"></a>Integrate PDF files in your website</h2>
<p>Part of optimizing your PDF files is making them an integral part of your website. This means they have to be linked to from within your website too, just like regular pages. These internal links will pass on link authority to the PDF file, enabling it to rank for relevant keywords.</p>
<h2 id="prevent-duplicate-content-canonicalize-pdf-files">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#prevent-duplicate-content-canonicalize-pdf-files" tabindex="-1"></a>Prevent duplicate content: canonicalize PDF files</h2>
<p>Remember, PDF files are treated as normal pages so that means they can cause duplicate content too. A pages and a PDF file can compete with one another just like two pages. In case the PDF file is a copy of content that's available elsewhere, be sure to <a href="/academy/canonical/#http-header">apply the canonical URL via the HTTP header</a> to signal search engines about this.</p>
<h2 id="dont-save-pdf-files-as-images">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#dont-save-pdf-files-as-images" tabindex="-1"></a>Don't save PDF files as images</h2>
<p>Often applications that allow you to create PDFs offer you to save the PDF as an image. While this allows visitors to still read the contents it will prevent them from selecting text. For search engines it's much more limiting: they'll have a hard time understanding the content of the PDF file. That's why it's important to save the PDF file with textual content, not with image content.</p>
<h2 id="optimize-pdf-files-for-mobile">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#optimize-pdf-files-for-mobile" tabindex="-1"></a>Optimize PDF files for Mobile</h2>
<p>At this point, there's no such thing as a responsive PDF file. You can left align the content of the PDF file as much as possible, thereby decreasing horizontal scrolling on mobile. Imagine having an image on the left hand side, and text on the right hand side. On a desktop this may look just fine, but on mobile it would be better to have the image above the text so visitors can just scroll vertically.</p>
<h2 id="optimize-pdf-file-size">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#optimize-pdf-file-size" tabindex="-1"></a>Optimize PDF file size</h2>
<p>Be mindful about the size of your PDF files. Not just for mobile visitors, this is an important best practice in general. Be mindful about using high quality images in the first place, and compress images if you find your PDF file to be larger than 5 MB.</p>

<h3 id="analytical-cookies">Analytical cookies</h3>
<p>We use analytical cookies in order to collect statistics about the use and visits of the Website. These analytic cookies generate statistical and other information about website use by means of cookies, which are stored on users’ Devices. The information generated relating to our Website is used to create reports about the use of the Website. Analytical cookies may be placed without your consent, unless such cookies have an impact on your privacy. In such cases, prior consent will be asked for.</p>
<h3 id="google-analytics">Google Analytics</h3>
<p>We use Google Analytics cookies in order to collect statistics about the use and visits of the Website. With these analytical cookies a permanent cookie is saved on your device to register your use of the Website. Google Analytics analyses this data and provides us with the results. This way, we obtain insight into the traffic of the Website and the way in which the Website is used. Based on this information we are able to make specific adjustments to the Website.</p>
<p>The information that we obtain is transferred to Google and stored by Google on servers outside the European Economic Area. We have entered into a data processing agreement with Google in which agreements have been made regarding the processing of personal data.</p>
<p>If you would like more information about analytical cookies and their expiry date, please visit the Google Analytics <a href="https://developers.google.com/analytics/devguides/collection/gajs/cookie-usage">information page</a>. Google also offers an <a href="https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout?hl=en">opt-out option</a> for data collection within the scope of Google Analytics.</p>
<h3 id="marketing-cookies">Marketing cookies</h3>
<p>We use third party marketing cookies in order to follow your internet browsing behavior and collect data and information on your browsing behavior from various websites you visited. This information is used to make the content of the displayed advertisements as relevant as possible, and to limit repetition of the same advertisements.</p>
<h3 id="social-plug-ins">Social plug-ins</h3>
<p>By using the Website you can get access to social media websites, such as Facebook, Twitter and LinkedIn. These buttons come with a code which has been made by these networks themselves. Using these so called social plug-ins you can login to your social media profile and subsequently share information from the Website with others. With these social plug-ins third party marketing cookies are saved on your Devices. These cookies serve the purpose of optimizing your user experience.</p>
<p>Companies such as LinkedIn, Twitter and Facebook may share your personal data outside the European Economic Area. Please read our Privacy Policy <a href="/legal/privacy-policy/">here</a> and the respective social network’s privacy statement to see how these companies treat your (personal) data.</p>
<h3 id="pixels">Pixels</h3>
<p>We also place pixels of third parties. A pixel keeps track of your surfing behavior and how you use the Website. These data are aggregated and give us information about the target group that visits our website. Based on this information, we can show an advertisement to a specific or similar target group on the website of the third party that produced the pixel.</p>
