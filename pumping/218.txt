<h1 class="heading__title">Biden and Harris have cleaned house at the White House</h1>
<div class="heading__params">
<ul class="list-of-params">
<li class="list-of-params__item">
<div aria-label="The publication date" class="heading__caption">
											January 26, 2021
										</div>
</li>
<li class="list-of-params__item">
<div aria-label="Author of article" class="heading__author">Steven van Vessum
										</div>
</li>
</ul>

<div class="post-detail__text typo">
<p>On January 20th 2021, President Biden was inaugurated. And soon after he and Vice-President Kamala Harris took their oath of office, they got to work. One of the first things they did was to launch the new Whitehouse.gov website.</p>
<p>We’re calling it a “new website,” because that’s what it is: while still powered by WordPress, it’s vastly different from the old one. The new administration has seriously cleaned house — removing thousands of pages — and they’ve adopted the motto of “less is more”.</p>
<p>We also detected some interesting last-minute tweaks made by the Trump administration whilst beating their retreat from office (more on that further down).</p>
<p>We’ll cover the most noteworthy changes since last week, as well as what changes we’re seeing as we’re typing this up.</p>
<p>Grab a cup of covfefe, and dig in!</p>
<h2 id="alerts-indicating-a-massive-shift-in-url-types">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#alerts-indicating-a-massive-shift-in-url-types" tabindex="-1"></a>Alerts indicating a massive shift in URL types</h2>
<p>First of all: how did we end up here?</p>
<p>Our real-time monitoring platform <a href="/features/seo-alerting/">sends alerts</a> in cases of high-impact changes and issues. It’s an “early warning system for SEO” so to speak. We’ve been monitoring the White House website for science, and on January 20th, we received an alert:</p>
<div class="media-container" style="max-width: 571px;"><a class="media-link" href="/features/seo-alerting/" target="_self"><picture><source srcset="/media/content/contentking-biden-harris-cleaned-house-seo-alert@1x.avif" type="image/avif"/><source srcset="/media/content/contentking-biden-harris-cleaned-house-seo-alert@1x.webp" type="image/webp"/><img alt="Screenshot of ContentKing Alert Pages Changed Type for Whitehouse.gov" class="image" height="513" src="/media/content/contentking-biden-harris-cleaned-house-seo-alert@1x.png" title="Screenshot of ContentKing Alert Pages Changed Type for Whitehouse.gov" width="571"/></picture></a></div>
<p>We knew of course that the inauguration would be taking place that day, but we still hadn’t anticipated a seismic shift like this.</p>
<p>We started digging in, and soon found out that thousands of pages had been removed:</p>
<div class="media-container" style="max-width: 600px;"><picture><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/content/contentking-biden-harris-cleaned-house-page-changes@1x.avif" type="image/avif"/><source srcset="/media/content/contentking-biden-harris-cleaned-house-page-changes@1x.avif, /media/content/contentking-biden-harris-cleaned-house-page-changes@2x.avif 2x" type="image/avif"/><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/content/contentking-biden-harris-cleaned-house-page-changes@1x.webp" type="image/webp"/><source srcset="/media/content/contentking-biden-harris-cleaned-house-page-changes@1x.webp, /media/content/contentking-biden-harris-cleaned-house-page-changes@2x.webp 2x" type="image/webp"/><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/content/contentking-biden-harris-cleaned-house-page-changes@1x.png"/><img alt="Screenshot from ContentKing's Page Changes visualisation" class="image" height="246" loading="lazy" src="/media/content/contentking-biden-harris-cleaned-house-page-changes@1x.png" srcset="/media/content/contentking-biden-harris-cleaned-house-page-changes@1x.png, /media/content/contentking-biden-harris-cleaned-house-page-changes@2x.png 2x" title="Screenshot from ContentKing's Page Changes visualisation" width="600"/></picture></div>
<p>We dug in further, while documenting what we came across as we went down this rabbit hole.</p>

<h2 id="the-biden-harris-administration-began-with-a-clean-slate">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#the-biden-harris-administration-began-with-a-clean-slate" tabindex="-1"></a>The Biden-Harris administration began with a clean slate</h2>
<p>The number of pages on whitehouse.gov literally went from <mark>11,500 unique pages to around 250</mark>. The new administration started with a clean slate, and only retained a portion of the “legacy” content about past administrations and The White House.</p>
<p>To give you an idea of the state of the website on January 19th:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/briefings-statements/</code> contained 7,500 pages.</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/presidential-actions/</code> contained 2,000 pages.</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/remarks/</code> and <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/news/</code> contained 1,000 paginated pages.</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/articles/</code> contained 700 pages.</li>
</ul>
<p>All the pages within these subfolders were removed and <strong>not redirected</strong>; only the parent URLs were redirected.</p>
<p>For example, the following <a href="/academy/redirects/faq/301-redirect/">301 redirects</a> were implemented:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/briefing-statements/</code> → <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/briefing-room/</code>
</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/presidential-actions/</code> → <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/briefing-room/presidential-actions/</code>
</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/remarks/</code> → <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/briefing-room/speeches-remarks/</code>
</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/articles/</code> → <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/briefing-room/</code>
</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="consolidation">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#consolidation" tabindex="-1"></a>Consolidation</h2>
<p>The new website’s information architecture is more hierarchical and organized.</p>
<p>For example, there’s one single place where you’ll find news — in the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/briefing-room/</code> section.</p>
<p>Old sections such as <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/briefings-statements/</code>, <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/1600daily/</code>, <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/articles/</code> all redirect to the newly created <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/briefing-room/</code> section.</p>
<p>The <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/briefing-room/</code> has the following sub-sections:</p>
<ul>
<li>Legislation — <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/briefing-room/legislation/</code>
</li>
<li>Presidential Actions — <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/briefing-room/presidential-actions/</code>
</li>
<li>Press Briefings — <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/briefing-room/press-briefings/</code>
</li>
<li>Speeches and Remarks — <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/briefing-room/speeches-remarks/</code>
</li>
<li>Statements and Releases — <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/briefing-room/statements-releases/</code>
</li>
</ul>
<p>A new sub-folder of “About the White House” called “The Grounds” was introduced. Its URL is <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/about-the-white-house/the-grounds/</code>. It contains all sorts of content about the buildings and facilities of the White House.</p>
<p>For example, the Air Force One page now lives there: <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/about-the-white-house/the-grounds/air-force-one/</code> while  the Camp David page now lives on <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/about-the-white-house/the-grounds/camp-david/</code>.</p>
<h2 id="less-is-more">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#less-is-more" tabindex="-1"></a>Less is more</h2>
<p>The entire “issues” section, listing 15 different issue-related topics, has been removed and now redirects to <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/priorities/</code>.</p>
<p>While a handful of issues are described on the “Priorities” page, there’s just one sub-page: “COVID-19”.</p>
<h2 id="a-future-proof-url-structure">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#a-future-proof-url-structure" tabindex="-1"></a>A future-proof URL structure</h2>
<p>Some parts of the old URL structure were hard to work with for obvious reasons.</p>
<p>Take for example <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/the-trump-administration/</code>. This URL could no longer be used as soon as the new administration took office. It now redirects to <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/administration/</code>.</p>
<p>This new URL <em>can</em> be re-used by future administrations. And please note that the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">the-</code> part has been left out — a good thing, because both visitors and search engines prefer <a href="/academy/urls/#keep-short">shorter URLs over longer ones</a>.</p>
<p>The page about “The Cabinet”, which previously lived on <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/the-trump-administration/the-cabinet/</code> now redirects to <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/administration/cabinet/</code>.</p>
<h3 id="the-new-url-structure-is-not-perfect-though">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#the-new-url-structure-is-not-perfect-though" tabindex="-1"></a>The new URL structure is not perfect though</h3>
<p>The new URL structure does make some URLs unnecessarily long-winded. That’s a missed opportunity — the new administration started with a blank slate, but it made some mistakes along the way.</p>
<p>Here are some examples:</p>
<ul>
<li>The URL for the “About” subfolder is <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/about-the-white-house/</code>, but in my opinion just /about/ would have sufficed. We’re on The White House website after all.</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/omb/information-regulatory-affairs/federal-collection-information/</code> was fine, but its new version lives on <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/omb/information-and-regulatory-affairs/federal-collection-information/</code>. The only change here is that <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/information-regulatory-affairs/</code> was changed to <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/information-and-regulatory-affairs/</code>.</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="noteworthy-content-was-removed">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#noteworthy-content-was-removed" tabindex="-1"></a>Noteworthy content was removed</h2>
<p>On top of the removed content we already covered in section “The Biden-Harris administration started with a clean slate”, here are other noteworthy pages and sections that were removed:</p>
<ul>
<li>The <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/people/</code> subfolder — containing 30 biographies, ranging from President Trump (<a href="https://trumpwhitehouse.archives.gov/people/donald-j-trump/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="extlink">link to archive</span><span class="show-for-sr"> (opens in a new tab)</span></a>) to Betsy DeVos.</li>
<li>Article “Operation Warp Speed Delivers Best Early Vaccination Rate of the G20”, published on January 15th (<a href="https://trumpwhitehouse.archives.gov/articles/operation-warp-speed-delivers-best-early-vaccination-rate-g20/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="extlink">link to archive</span><span class="show-for-sr"> (opens in a new tab)</span></a>).</li>
<li>“Trump Administration Accomplishments”, only just updated on January 15th (<a href="https://trumpwhitehouse.archives.gov/trump-administration-accomplishments/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="extlink">link to archive</span><span class="show-for-sr"> (opens in a new tab)</span></a>).</li>
<li>Medals of Freedom that were awarded by President Trump (<a href="https://trumpwhitehouse.archives.gov/medaloffreedom/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="extlink">link to archive</span><span class="show-for-sr"> (opens in a new tab)</span></a>).</li>
<li>First Lady Melania Trump’s “Be Best” Initiative (<a href="https://trumpwhitehouse.archives.gov/bebest/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="extlink">link to archive</span><span class="show-for-sr"> (opens in a new tab)</span></a>).</li>
</ul>
<p>And the “Miscellaneous” pages below were removed too:</p>
<ul>
<li>The “Turkey Pardoning” page, which had just been updated on January 16th (<a href="https://trumpwhitehouse.archives.gov/gobble/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="extlink">link to archive</span><span class="show-for-sr"> (opens in a new tab)</span></a>).</li>
<li>Christmas Season at the White House, also only just updated on January 16th (<a href="https://trumpwhitehouse.archives.gov/christmas/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="extlink">link to archive</span><span class="show-for-sr"> (opens in a new tab)</span></a>).</li>
<li>The White House Easter Egg Roll (<a href="https://trumpwhitehouse.archives.gov/eastereggroll/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="extlink">link to archive</span><span class="show-for-sr"> (opens in a new tab)</span></a>).</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="noteworthy-content-was-added">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#noteworthy-content-was-added" tabindex="-1"></a>Noteworthy content was added</h2>
<p>The new site launch added new content too. And some of it is especially noteworthy.</p>
<h3 id="spanish-content">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#spanish-content" tabindex="-1"></a>Spanish content</h3>
<p>It’s fascinating that the new administration published 41 new pages in Spanish. In the top right corner, there’s a link to the Spanish version of the site — “La Casa Blanca”.</p>
<p>For example, President Biden's page is marked up with the following hreflang:</p>
<div class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge"><div class="highlight"><pre class="highlight"><code>&lt;link rel="alternate" hreflang="en-US" href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/administration/president-biden/"&gt;
&lt;link rel="alternate" hreflang="es-MX" href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/es/administracion/presidente-biden/"&gt;
</code></pre></div></div>
<p>Interestingly, the <a href="/academy/hreflang/">hreflang value</a> for the Spanish content — <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">es-MX</code> — is targeting Spanish speakers within Mexico. Not Spanish speakers all across the globe, or within the US (which the value <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">es-US</code> could have been used for).</p>
<p>The <a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/es/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="extlink">Spanish homepage</span><span class="show-for-sr"> (opens in a new tab)</span></a> doesn’t contain any hreflang markup though, which to us seems like a bug, as it’s a localized version of the regular homepage.</p>
<h3 id="more-focus-on-accessibility">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#more-focus-on-accessibility" tabindex="-1"></a>More focus on accessibility</h3>

<p>While the idea is good, the Accessibility Statement page itself — and other pages — leave much to be desired.</p>

<h2 id="the-404-page">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#the-404-page" tabindex="-1"></a>The 404 page</h2>
<p>With so much content that’s been removed, and millions of people hitting the 404 page, the link to the <a href="https://www.archives.gov/presidential-libraries/archived-websites" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="extlink">archived Presidential White House Websites</span><span class="show-for-sr"> (opens in a new tab)</span></a> is useful indeed.</p>
<p>If we could suggest one more improvement there, we’d include the direct link to the archived version of the page you hit a 404 page for.</p>
<p>For example, if you were looking up the “Turkey Pardoning” page, you’d appreciate a direct link to the archived version of that page, instead of the overview of past Presidential White House websites.</p>
<h2 id="hidden-recruitment-message-in-pages-source">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#hidden-recruitment-message-in-pages-source" tabindex="-1"></a>Hidden recruitment message in page’s source</h2>

<p><code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">&lt;!-- If you're reading this, we need your help building back better. https://usds.gov/ --&gt;</code></p>

<h2 id="some-next-steps-for-better-seo-performance">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#some-next-steps-for-better-seo-performance" tabindex="-1"></a>Some next steps for better SEO performance</h2>
<p>As with any <a href="/academy/website-migrations/">website migration</a>, there is always a huge list of things to fix post-launch. This migration is no different. Here are the most important things for them to work on right now in my opinion:</p>
<h3 id="implement-redirects">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#implement-redirects" tabindex="-1"></a>Implement redirects</h3>
<p>Whitehouse.gov is one of the most authoritative domains in the world, so you’d think they wouldn’t need the authority that’s going down the drain due to all the linked content that’s been removed.</p>
<p>But what if they wanted to rank quickly with new content, and they know they have several well linked, highly related articles that were removed?</p>
<p>We would definitely implement 301-redirects to the newly published content.</p>
<p>We wouldn’t implement blanket 301 redirects, because that would lead to <a href="/academy/index-coverage/faq/submitted-soft-404/">soft 404 issues</a> but we’d do content inventory on the content that’s been removed and seek out highly-relevant counterparts on the new website.</p>
<h3 id="broken-links">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#broken-links" tabindex="-1"></a>Broken links</h3>
<p>The site contains a lot of broken links — some on very important pages. Take for example <a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/ostp/legal/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="extlink">Legal</span><span class="show-for-sr"> (opens in a new tab)</span></a> — most documents that are linked there return 404 errors.</p>
<h3 id="links-to-redirecting-pages">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#links-to-redirecting-pages" tabindex="-1"></a>Links to redirecting pages</h3>
<p>There’s a lot of incorrect links embedded in body content where the trailing slash (the last slash of a URL) is missing. The site automatically redirects clients to the version <em>with</em> a trailing slash, but it’s best to just directly link to the correct URL. This reduces the page load time.</p>
<p>For example, on the Legal page, there is a link to the Contact page, but instead of linking to <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">https://www.whitehouse.gov/contact/</code>, they’re linking to <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">https://www.whitehouse.gov/contact</code>, with the strange anchor text <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">http://whitehouse.gov/contact</code>:</p>

<h3 id="xml-sitemap">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#xml-sitemap" tabindex="-1"></a>XML Sitemap</h3>
<p>We recommend adding <a href="/academy/xml-sitemap/">an XML sitemap</a> that contains the Spanish content. If it’s really important, this content should be listed in an XML sitemap too. And that XML sitemap should be submitted to Google Search Console.</p>
<p>Additionally, we’d disable the author pages if they aren’t used — the “White House” author page <a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/briefing-room/author/whitehouse/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="extlink">returns a 404 right now</span><span class="show-for-sr"> (opens in a new tab)</span></a>.
