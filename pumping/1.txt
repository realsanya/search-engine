<h1 class="heading__title">Title tag</h1>
<div class="heading__params">
<span aria-hidden="true" class="heading__label">
										Last updated:
									</span>
<span aria-label="The date of last update" class="heading__caption">
										September 13, 2021
									</span>
</div>
</div>
<div class="post-detail__text typo">
<section aria-label="The Title Tag in short" class="highlighted-box highlighted-box--with-background typo-block"><div aria-hidden="true" class="highlighted-box__title">
<span class="highlighted-box__title-icon"><i class="post-type-icon post-type-icon--reversed-style" style="background-image: url(/assets/images/icons/post-types/title--reversed.svg)"></i></span>The Title Tag in short
	</div>
<div class="highlighted-box__content">
<p>The title tag describes the main topic of a page in one brief sentence. It's shown in search engine result pages, and is considered the most important on-page SEO element.</p>
<p>Keep the following things in mind when writing titles:</p>
<ul>
<li>Keep its length between 285 pixels and 575 pixels (± 30 and 60 characters respectively).</li>
<li>Place the most important keywords at the beginning of the title.</li>
<li>Make sure that the title is easy to read, and that they are unique across your entire website</li>
</ul>
</div>
</section>
<div aria-label="Table of contents" class="highlighted-box" role="navigation">
<div aria-hidden="true" class="highlighted-box__title">Table of contents</div>
<div class="highlighted-box__content">
<ul class="navigation-list">
<li class="navigation-list__item navigation-list__item--primary"><a href="#what-is-title-tag">What is a title tag?</a></li>
<li class="navigation-list__item navigation-list__item--primary"><a href="#writing-the-perfect-title-tag">How do I write the perfect title tag?</a></li>
<li class="navigation-list__item navigation-list__item--primary"><a href="#faq">Frequently asked questions about title tags</a></li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
<section aria-label="Recent update to Google’s title system " class="highlighted-box typo-block highlighted-box--with-background"><div aria-hidden="true" class="highlighted-box__title">Recent update to Google’s title system </div>
<div class="highlighted-box__content">
<p>In August 2021, <a href="/blog/google-title-update/">Google updated</a> how web page titles are generated and shown in search results. The title update introduced a new system, which focuses primarily on the main visual title or headline of the page to generate a title.</p>
<p>With this update, it's become increasingly important to craft your own high-quality titles that fit with recommended guidelines — this is titles that are highly relevant and the proper length. By not doing so, it's more likely that titles will be rewritten.</p>
</div></section>
<h2 id="what-is-title-tag">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#what-is-title-tag" tabindex="-1"></a>What is a title tag?</h2>
<p>When people refer to the title tag they often mean the <em>title element</em>. The title element defines the title of a page.</p>
<h3 id="example">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#example" tabindex="-1"></a>Example</h3>
<p>Let’s take a look at the ContentKing homepage title. In a Google search engine result page (SERP) the title is shown as the line at the top, marked with a red square in the screenshot below:</p>

</code></pre></div></div>
<h3 id="rule-of-thumb-for-title-tag-length">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#rule-of-thumb-for-title-tag-length" tabindex="-1"></a>Rule of thumb for title tag length</h3>
<p>With Google's Title update, shorter titles are preferred. If the title is too long, they are more liklely to be rewritten.</p>
<p>Use these rules of thumb when it comes to the title tag length:</p>
<ul>
<li>Maximum length: <strong>60 characters</strong> or <strong>575 pixels</strong>
</li>
<li>Minimum length: <strong>30 characters</strong> or <strong>285 pixels</strong>
</li>
</ul>
<p>These lengths are valid in most cases for most devices for search engines Google, Bing, Yahoo and DuckDuckGo. However, in the end these lengths are no guarantee that your title will be displayed and it’s up to the search engines to decide.</p>
<h3 id="synonyms-for-title-tag">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#synonyms-for-title-tag" tabindex="-1"></a>Synonyms for title tag</h3>
<p>Often the title tag is also called page title, meta title or - because of its relevance to SEO - SEO title. For the sake of ease, we’ll call it <strong>title tag</strong> when we refer to the title element.</p>
<p>Although the title tag is often referred to as "meta title", by definition, the title tag is not a <a href="/academy/meta-tags/#meta-title">meta tag</a>.</p>

<h2 id="writing-the-perfect-title-tag">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#writing-the-perfect-title-tag" tabindex="-1"></a>How do I write the perfect title tag?</h2>
<p>The perfect title tag consists of the following elements:</p>
<ul>
<li>Great readability</li>
<li>Relevant keywords</li>
<li>Call-to-action</li>
<li>Good length</li>
<li>Uniqueness</li>
</ul>
<p>If you keep these components in mind, you’ll get the most out of your title tags. Before we dive into what makes for a great title tag, let’s take a minute and look at an example.</p>
<h3 id="example-law-firm-new-york">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#example-law-firm-new-york" tabindex="-1"></a>Example: Law Firm New York</h3>
<p>One of the results I received from Google when searching for <strong>“law firm new york”</strong> is:</p>
<div class="media-container" style="max-width: 507px;"><img alt="Example of Law Firm title" class="image" height="80" loading="lazy" src="/wp-content/uploads/2016/05/image00.png" title="Example of Law Firm title" width="507"/></div>
<p>This title tag can be improved greatly. Our assessment of the title tag:</p>
<ul>
<li>Readability: the readability of the title is so-so. Having a dash (<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">-</code>) and pipe (<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">|</code>)in the title tag decreases readability. On top of that, the title is truncated which hurts the readability as well.</li>
<li>Relevant keywords: the title tag is not very relevant - what does “Canada’s Top Business Law Firm” have to do with your search query? Besides, the keyword usage in the title tag is not good - the applicable keywords are at the end.</li>
<li>Call-to-action: in this case, the call-to-action requirement doesn’t really apply.</li>
<li>Length: the title tag is too long. Google truncated the title in the example above.</li>
</ul>
<p>Wouldn’t the title tag below be much better? We even squeezed in “NYC” as a tertiary keyword.</p>
<div class="media-container" style="max-width: 504px;"><img alt="Improved example of Law Firm title" class="image" height="78" loading="lazy" src="/wp-content/uploads/2016/05/image01.png" title="Improved example of Law Firm title" width="504"/></div>
<p><strong>Keep in mind:</strong> Write title tags primarily for users, not for search engines. Think about the most important search queries that apply to a page, and then write the title.</p>
<h3 id="great-readability">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#great-readability" tabindex="-1"></a>Great readability</h3>
<p>Focus on the user when writing title tags: first and foremost the title should be easy to read.</p>
<ul>
<li>Avoid title tags written in CAPITALS or lowercase only. Mix it and use uppercase letters to emphasize parts of the title tag.</li>
<li>Avoid repeating special characters such as dashes (<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">-</code>), pipes (<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">|</code>) or commas (<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">,</code>) in title tags.</li>
</ul>
<p>Write a title tag that’s informative and scannable for the user.</p>
<p>Needless to say, a title tag such as the one below has a very poor readability:</p>
<div class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge"><div class="highlight"><pre class="highlight"><code>Pink shoes &amp;amp; Blue shoes | Yellow shoes - Shoes for men &amp;amp; women | Shoes.com
</code></pre></div></div>
<h3 id="relevant-keywords">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#relevant-keywords" tabindex="-1"></a>Relevant keywords</h3>
<p>First of all: don’t stuff your titles with keywords. Doing so will dilute keyword focus and you may end up not ranking for any of the keywords at all. Make choices.</p>
<p>Using the most important keywords at the start of your title tag signals to search engines that those are the most relevant keywords for the page in question.</p>
<p>Ideally, you use one or two keywords and write an appealing title. Think in terms of keyphrases instead of separate keywords. Are the most important keywords for your page <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Content optimization</code> and <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Optimization service</code> (in that order)? Then combine these keywords to the keyphrase <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Content optimization service…</code> instead of <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Content optimization, optimization service…</code>.</p>
<p>Sometimes however it’s simply not possible to construct a keyphrase, and you need to fall back to a general title tag structure such as:</p>
<ul>
<li>Example #1: <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Primary keyword, secondary keyword | Brand</code>
</li>
<li>Example #2: <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Primary keyword and secondary keyword - Brand</code>
</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="include-a-call-to-action">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#include-a-call-to-action" tabindex="-1"></a>Include a Call-To-Action</h3>
<p>Choose relevant keywords is important, but choosing the right <em>words</em> for the rest of the title is just as important. Carefully chosen words will activate a potential customer.</p>
<h4 id="examples">Examples:</h4>
<ul>
<li>Are you running a webshop? Include the words <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Buy</code> or <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Order</code>.</li>
<li>Are you running a consultancy firm? Include the word <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Hire</code> in the title tag.</li>
<li>Is your website educational? Include the word <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Learn</code>.</li>
<li>Is your page about a webinar or newsletter? Include the word <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Subscribe</code>.</li>
</ul>
<p>Think about words that will activate your target group and incorporate these words in the title.</p>
<h3 id="good-length">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#good-length" tabindex="-1"></a>Good length</h3>
<p>There is no perfect title tag length. Title tag length is actually a very complex subject because you need to take into account different search engines, operating systems and devices. This section describes all you need to know about the title tag length.</p>
<h4 id="characters-vs-pixels">Characters vs. Pixels</h4>
<p>Regardless of the search engine, in each SERP there’s limited amount of space for the title. Bing, Yahoo and DuckDuckGo advise to use a maximum amount of characters. Google however advises to use a <strong>maximum amount of pixels</strong>. This makes sense considering this is way more accurate than characters. It takes into consideration that a character such as “W” takes up much more space than “!”.</p>
<h4 id="title-tag-length-in-google">Title tag length in Google</h4>
<p>A listing in Google’s SERPs has a maximum width of 600 pixels. Taking into account some margin of error, <strong>we advise you to stick to a maximum length of 575 pixels</strong>. In general this translates into 60 characters.</p>
<p>In case your title is too short, Google may use one of your <a href="/academy/headings/">headings</a> instead. In order to prevent this, make sure your title tag is at least 30 characters long.</p>
<h5 id="in-summary-title-tag-length-for-google">In summary: title tag length for Google</h5>
<ul>
<li>Maximum amount: 60 characters or 575 pixels</li>
<li>Minimum amount: 30 characters or 285 pixels</li>
</ul>
<p>Please note that Google’s tablet and mobile versions have a greater maximum width for the title tag, however it’s not (yet) possible to set specific title tags for different devices, so we recommend to stick to the maximum width for desktop to make sure the title tag adheres to Google’s constraints as much as possible.</p>
<h4 id="title-tag-length-in-bing">Title tag length in Bing</h4>
<p><a href="https://www.bing.com/webmaster/help/webmaster-guidelines-30fba23a" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="extlink">Bing</span><span class="show-for-sr"> (opens in a new tab)</span></a> advises to use a maximum title tag length of 65 characters. Our own research has shown that titles with 65 characters are often cut-off in Bing, so we advise to keep it to a maximum of 60 characters when you’re optimizing for Bing specifically. In case your title is too short, Bing may use one of your headings instead. In order to prevent this, make sure your title tag is at least 25 characters long.</p>
<p>Please note that for Bing there are no different rules per device regarding the maximum lengths of the title tag.</p>
<h4 id="title-tag-length-in-yahoo">Title tag length in Yahoo</h4>
<p>Yahoo does not give much <a href="https://help.yahoo.com/kb/web-page-title-description-incorrect-sln2195.html" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="extlink">advice in their Help section</span><span class="show-for-sr"> (opens in a new tab)</span></a> about the minimum and maximum length of the title tag. Our own research has shown that the Yahoo SERP allows for a maximum width of 553 pixels. This roughly translates to 65-70 characters. However, we haven’t seen this full width being used, nor have we seen title tags with this many characters being displayed. In practice a significant amount of title tags above 50 characters are truncated so we advise you to stick to a maximum amount of 50 characters. In case your title is too short, Yahoo may use one of your headings instead. In order to prevent this, make sure your title tag is at least 25 characters long.</p>
<p>Please note that for Yahoo there are no different rules per device regarding the maximum lengths of the title tag.</p>
<h4 id="title-tag-length-in-duckduckgo">Title tag length in DuckDuckGo</h4>
<p>Although DuckDuckGo is not very informative about their advice on how to optimize pages for their search engine, our research has shown that when you stick to a maximum of 65 characters you should be safe. In case your title is too short, DuckDuckGo may use one of your headings instead. In order to prevent this, make sure your title tag is at least 25 characters long.</p>
<h3 id="unique-title-tags-throughout-a-website">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#unique-title-tags-throughout-a-website" tabindex="-1"></a>Unique title tags throughout a website</h3>
<p>It’s vital that each page has a unique title tag. Having duplicate title tags is confusing for both users and search engines because the title is an important differentiator. Especially search engines will have trouble picking the right page to rank for incorporated keywords, often only ranking and displaying one. Don't rely on solutions such as the <a href="/academy/canonical/">canonical URL</a> to fix this for you.</p>

<h2 id="what-are-cookies">What are cookies?</h2>
<p>Cookies are small text files which are stored on the browser or hard drive of your Device when you visit a webpage or use an application. Cookies may be needed to show the webpage or application on your Device and are also used to enhance the user experience. Cookies cannot damage your Devices or the files saved on it. There are different types of cookies. Some cookies come directly from our website (first party cookies) and others come from third parties which place cookies on our site (third party cookies).</p>
<p>Cookies can be stored for varying lengths of time on your browser or device. Session cookies are deleted from your computer or device when you close your web-browser. Persistent cookies will remain stored on your computer or Device until deleted or until they reach their expiry date.</p>
<h2 id="how-do-we-use-cookies">How do we use cookies?</h2>
<h3 id="essential-technical-cookies">Essential (Technical) cookies</h3>
<p>We try to provide an advanced and user-friendly website that adapts automatically to needs and wishes of our visitors and users. To achieve this, we use technical cookies to i.e. show you our Website, to make it function correctly, to create your ContentKing account, to sign you in and to manage your requests. These technical cookies are necessary for our website to function properly.</p>
<h3 id="functional-cookies">Functional cookies</h3>
<p>We also use functional cookies to remember your preferences and settings (as username, password, language etc.) and to help you to use the Website efficiently and effectively. These functional cookies are not strictly necessary for the functioning of our Website, but they add functionality for you and enhance your experience.</p>
<h3 id="analytical-cookies">Analytical cookies</h3>
<p>We use analytical cookies in order to collect statistics about the use and visits of the Website. These analytic cookies generate statistical and other information about website use by means of cookies, which are stored on users’ Devices. The information generated relating to our Website is used to create reports about the use of the Website. Analytical cookies may be placed without your consent, unless such cookies have an impact on your privacy. In such cases, prior consent will be asked for.</p>
<h3 id="google-analytics">Google Analytics</h3>
<p>We use Google Analytics cookies in order to collect statistics about the use and visits of the Website. With these analytical cookies a permanent cookie is saved on your device to register your use of the Website. Google Analytics analyses this data and provides us with the results. This way, we obtain insight into the traffic of the Website and the way in which the Website is used. Based on this information we are able to make specific adjustments to the Website.</p>
<p>The information that we obtain is transferred to Google and stored by Google on servers outside the European Economic Area. We have entered into a data processing agreement with Google in which agreements have been made regarding the processing of personal data.</p>
<p>If you would like more information about analytical cookies and their expiry date, please visit the Google Analytics <a href="https://developers.google.com/analytics/devguides/collection/gajs/cookie-usage">information page</a>. Google also offers an <a href="https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout?hl=en">opt-out option</a> for data collection within the scope of Google Analytics.</p>
<h3 id="marketing-cookies">Marketing cookies</h3>
<p>We use third party marketing cookies in order to follow your internet browsing behavior and collect data and information on your browsing behavior from various websites you visited. This information is used to make the content of the displayed advertisements as relevant as possible, and to limit repetition of the same advertisements.</p>

<p>By using the Website you can get access to social media websites, such as Facebook, Twitter and LinkedIn. These buttons come with a code which has been made by these networks themselves. Using these so called social plug-ins you can login to your social media profile and subsequently share information from the Website with others. With these social plug-ins third party marketing cookies are saved on your Devices. These cookies serve the purpose of optimizing your user experience.</p>
<p>Companies such as LinkedIn, Twitter and Facebook may share your personal data outside the European Economic Area. Please read our Privacy Policy <a href="/legal/privacy-policy/">here</a> and the respective social network’s privacy statement to see how these companies treat your (personal) data.</p>
<h3 id="pixels">Pixels</h3>
<p>We also place pixels of third parties. A pixel keeps track of your surfing behavior and how you use the Website. These data are aggregated and give us information about the target group that visits our website. Based on this information, we can show an advertisement to a specific or similar target group on the website of the third party that produced the pixel.</p>

<p>You can configure your browser so that you do not receive any cookies the next time you use the website. You can read here how you can remove different types of cookies in different browsers. Please consult the help-function of your browser if your browser is not listed below.</p>
