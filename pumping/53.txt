<span class="highlighted-box__title-icon"><i class="post-type-icon post-type-icon--reversed-style" style="background-image: url(/assets/images/icons/post-types/gsc-url-inspection-tool--reversed.svg)"></i></span>GSC URL Inspection Tool in short

<p>Every SEO should have Google Search Console, or “GSC” for short, in their SEO toolbox, as it provides them with essential information to understand how their website is performing in Google’s organic search results. If you haven’t checked your site in GSC yet, do it now!</p>
<p>In this article, we’ll dig into one of its core features—the URL inspection Tool—and describe how you can take advantage of its insights and features to improve your SEO game.</p>
<h2 id="what-is">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#what-is" tabindex="-1"></a>What is the URL Inspection Tool?</h2>
<p>The URL Inspection Tool enables you to diagnose SEO issues on your site by providing insights as to what information Google has on file about your URL’s accessibility and indexability, and by letting you test URLs live.</p>
<p>This tool is specifically designed to inspect web pages—not other file types such as PDFs, images, and videos.</p>
<p>The URL Inspection Tool and the <a href="/academy/index-coverage/">Index Coverage report</a> are the most helpful features in Google Search Console for diagnosing and fixing SEO issues.</p>

<h2 id="how-it-works">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#how-it-works" tabindex="-1"></a>How does the URL Inspection Tool work?</h2>
<p>Let’s run the URL Inspection Tool on one of our own articles and go through the results:</p>
<figure aria-labelledby="image-caption-1" class="captioned-image"><div class="media-container" style="max-width: 600px;"><picture><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-explained@1x.avif" type="image/avif"/><source srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-explained@1x.avif, /media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-explained@2x.avif 2x" type="image/avif"/><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-explained@1x.webp" type="image/webp"/><source srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-explained@1x.webp, /media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-explained@2x.webp 2x" type="image/webp"/><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-explained@1x.png"/><img alt="Interface screenshot from Google Search Console’s URL Inspection tool" class="image" height="437" src="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-explained@1x.png" srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-explained@1x.png, /media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-explained@2x.png 2x" title="Interface screenshot from Google Search Console’s URL Inspection tool" width="600"/></picture></div>
<figcaption class="captioned-image__caption" id="image-caption-1">Results from inspecting one of our articles with the URL Inspection Tool.</figcaption></figure>
<p>Let’s explain what you’re seeing step-by-step:</p>
<ol>
<li>This article’s <a href="/academy/url-inspection-tool/#presence-on-google">Presence on Google</a> status is <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">URL is on Google</code>—this means it can appear in Google Search. That’s what you want to see!</li>
<li>
<a href="/academy/url-inspection-tool/#view-crawled-page">View crawled page</a> lets you see the HTML response Google received after requesting the article, along with more technical details such as the HTTP response code that was received.</li>
<li>On the right, you see the <a href="/academy/url-inspection-tool/#request-indexing">Request Indexing</a> button, which you can use to get Google to re-crawl and re-index your URL.</li>
<li>Under <a href="/academy/url-inspection-tool/#coverage">Coverage</a>, you’ll find more detailed information about whether Google was able to crawl and index the URL properly. In our case, it says <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Submitted and indexed</code>, meaning we submitted the URL through our XML sitemap, and it was subsequently indexed.</li>
<li>Under <a href="/academy/url-inspection-tool/#enhancements">Enhancements</a>, Google reports whether it encountered any Mobile Usability issues, what Schema markup has been found, and whether it’s valid. In our case, it all looks good and matches what we’d expect.</li>
<li>The <a href="/academy/url-inspection-tool/#test-live-url">Test Live URL</a> feature lets you run a live test on this URL. This feature is useful if you want to validate fixes or changes.</li>
</ol>
<section aria-label="Pro tip" class="highlighted-box typo-block highlighted-box--with-icon highlighted-box--protip-style"><div aria-hidden="true" class="highlighted-box__title">Pro tip</div>
<div class="highlighted-box__content">
<p>Keep in mind that the information you’ll find in the URL Inspection Tool dates from the last time Google indexed or tried to index your URL. Use the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Test Live URL</code> feature to test the current status of the URL.</p>
</div></section>
<p>To get to the screen above for an URL of your own, go through these steps:</p>
<section class="highlighted-box typo-block highlighted-box--with-icon highlighted-box--steps-style"><div class="highlighted-box__content">
<ol>
<li>Log onto <a href="https://search.google.com/search-console" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="extlink">Google Search Console</span><span class="show-for-sr"> (opens in a new tab)</span></a>.</li>
<li>On the left, choose the URL property you want to inspect.</li>
<li>In the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Inspect any URL in “https://example.com/”</code> field at the top, enter the full text of the URL you want to inspect.</li>
</ol>

<p>Now for each of the sections described above, let’s explain what information you’ll find, what it means, and how you can leverage it to improve your SEO performance!</p>
<h3 class="toc" id="presence-on-google">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#presence-on-google" tabindex="-1"></a>1. Presence on Google</h3>
<div class="media-container" style="max-width: 600px;"><picture><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-section-presence-on-google@1x.avif" type="image/avif"/><source srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-section-presence-on-google@1x.avif, /media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-section-presence-on-google@2x.avif 2x" type="image/avif"/><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-section-presence-on-google@1x.webp" type="image/webp"/><source srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-section-presence-on-google@1x.webp, /media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-section-presence-on-google@2x.webp 2x" type="image/webp"/><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-section-presence-on-google@1x.png"/><img alt="Screenshot of the Presence on Google section of the URL Inspection tool" class="image" height="282" loading="lazy" src="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-section-presence-on-google@1x.png" srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-section-presence-on-google@1x.png, /media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-section-presence-on-google@2x.png 2x" title="Screenshot of the Presence on Google section of the URL Inspection tool" width="600"/></picture></div>
<p>Let’s start with the URL’s “presence status”, as highlighted in the screenshot above. There, you can encounter the following statuses:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/academy/url-inspection-tool/#url-is-on-google">URL is on Google</a></li>

<h4 id="url-is-on-google">URL is on Google</h4>
<p>This status is accompanied by the following explanation:</p>
<p>"<em>It can appear in Google Search results (if not subject to a manual action or removal request) with all relevant enhancements.</em>"</p>
<p><strong>What this means</strong><br/>
This is the status you’ll find if everything is fine with your URL.</p>
<p>And unless you’ve got a manual action (“manual Google penalty”) or submitted a removal request, this URL can appear in Google’s search results with all of its enhancements.</p>
<p>That doesn’t mean your URL is ranking for anything though. It just means it’s been crawled and indexed without problems and is eligible to rank. To find out if your URL is ranking, follow <a href="/academy/google-index/#index-check">these steps</a>.</p>
<section aria-label="Action required" class="highlighted-box typo-block highlighted-box--with-icon highlighted-box--action-style"><div aria-hidden="true" class="highlighted-box__title">Action required</div>
<div class="highlighted-box__content">
<p>With this status, usually no action is required.
If the URL isn’t ranking: tweak the content, add more internal links, and gain backlinks to improve its performance.</p>
</div></section>

<h4 id="url-is-on-google-but-issues">URL is on Google, but has issues</h4>
<p>This status is accompanied by the following explanation:</p>
<p>"<em>It can appear in Google Search results (if not subject to a manual action or removal request) with all relevant enhancements. However, some issues prevent it from being eligible for all enhancements.</em>"</p>
<p><strong>What this means</strong><br/>
Your URL was crawled and indexed, but Google has found issues with its enhancements. They may display just fine, or partially, or not at all—it depends on the enhancements’ issues.</p>
<p>For example, Google is quick to throw a warning for missing recommended fields with the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">JobPosting</code> Schema, and yet it will show the enhancement just fine.</p>
<p>Here again, unless you’ve got a manual action or submitted a removal request, this URL can appear in Google’s search results (but it may not be ranking).</p>
<section aria-label="Action required" class="highlighted-box typo-block highlighted-box--with-icon highlighted-box--action-style"><div aria-hidden="true" class="highlighted-box__title">Action required</div>
<div class="highlighted-box__content">
<p>Investigate what issues Google has found with the URL’s enhancements and fix these if needed. If they are just <em>warnings</em> that actually don’t impact your enhancements’ visibility, you can leave them be.</p>
<p>If you do need to fix something, use <a href="/academy/url-inspection-tool/#test-live-url">Test Live URL</a> afterwards to verify your fixes were correct.</p>
</div></section>
<h4 id="url-is-not-on-google">URL is not on Google</h4>
<p>This status is accompanied by the following explanation:</p>
<p>"<em>This page is not in the index, but not because of an error. See the details below to learn why it wasn't indexed.</em>"</p>
<p><strong>What this means</strong><br/>
Your URL was crawled by Google, but during indexing it concluded that it was probably not your intention to get it indexed. Common reasons why it would think that are: it was disallowed in your <a href="/academy/robotstxt/">robots.txt</a>, was behind <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">HTTP auth</code>, or had a <a href="/academy/meta-robots-tag/#noindex">noindex directive</a>.</p>

<p>If everything looks fine, it may just be a matter of time before Google re-crawls and indexes the URL. We’ve found Google also using the status <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">URL is not on Google</code> for 4xx errors and other crawl issues.</p>

<h4 id="url-is-not-on-google-indexing-errors">URL is not on Google: Indexing errors</h4>
<p>This status is accompanied by the following explanation:</p>
<p>"<em>It cannot appear in Google Search results until it’s indexed.</em>"</p>
<p><strong>What this means</strong><br/>
Google was unable to crawl and index your URL because it ran into indexing errors. For example, if the URL returned a <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">4xx</code> or <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">5xx</code> status code, it would end up with this status. This URL will not appear in Google’s search results.</p>
<section aria-label="Pro tip" class="highlighted-box typo-block highlighted-box--with-icon highlighted-box--protip-style"><div aria-hidden="true" class="highlighted-box__title">Pro tip</div>
<div class="highlighted-box__content">
<p>URLs that were crawlable and indexable previously, but now return <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">4xx</code> status codes, are given this status instead of <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">URL is not on Google</code>.</p>
</div></section>
<section aria-label="Action required" class="highlighted-box typo-block highlighted-box--with-icon highlighted-box--action-style"><div aria-hidden="true" class="highlighted-box__title">Action required</div>
<div class="highlighted-box__content">
<p>Assuming you want this URL to rank, investigate what’s preventing Google from crawling and indexing the URL. Double-check that Google has unrestricted access and ensure that nothing is keeping them from indexing the page. Then, use Test Live URL to verify.</p>
<p>Similar to the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">URL is not on Google</code> status, it may just be a matter of time before Google re-crawls and re-indexes the URL.</p>
</div></section>
<h4 id="url-is-an-alternate-version">URL is an alternate version</h4>
<p>This status is accompanied by the following explanation:</p>
<p>"<em>This is an AMP version of another URL, which can appear in mobile search results.</em>"</p>
<p><strong>What this means</strong><br/>
This means that the URL you entered is either the AMP version or the alternate mobile/desktop version of an URL. For instance, if your website is mobile-first and you entered the desktop URL, this is the status you’ll find. The alternate version can appear in the mobile search results, while the desktop version can appear in the desktop search results.</p>
<section aria-label="Action required" class="highlighted-box typo-block highlighted-box--with-icon highlighted-box--action-style"><div aria-hidden="true" class="highlighted-box__title">Action required</div>
<div class="highlighted-box__content">
<p>With this status, no action is required.</p>
</div></section>

<h4 id="view-crawled-page-more-info">Tab: More Info</h4>
<p>Under the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">More Info</code> tab, you’ll find more technical information:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<strong>Page type</strong>: shows the content type of the URL you’re inspecting. For pages, you’ll find <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">text/html</code> here, and when you’re inspecting a PDF, it’s <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">application/pdf</code>.</li>
<li>
<strong>HTTP response</strong>: shows the <a href="/academy/http-status-codes/">HTTP status code</a> the URL returned, and when you click on the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">⟩</code> you’ll see the full <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">HTTP headers</code>. Here you’ll for instance be able to spot any X-Robots-Tag header, canonical and hreflang implemented through the header, and caching guidelines.</li>
<li>
<strong>Page resources</strong>: when you click the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">⟩</code>, you’ll find a list of page resources that loaded, and those that didn’t. Examples of page resources are: JavaScript files, CSS files, and fonts.</li>
<li>
<strong>JavaScript console messages</strong>: this will always show <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Not available in indexed version</code>, as you’ll need to use <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Test Live URL</code> to gain information about this.</li>

<div class="media-container" style="max-width: 600px;"><picture><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-request-indexing@1x.avif" type="image/avif"/><source srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-request-indexing@1x.avif, /media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-request-indexing@2x.avif 2x" type="image/avif"/><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-request-indexing@1x.webp" type="image/webp"/><source srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-request-indexing@1x.webp, /media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-request-indexing@2x.webp 2x" type="image/webp"/><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-request-indexing@1x.png"/><img alt="Screenshot of Google Search Console’s Request Indexing feature" class="image" height="248" loading="lazy" src="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-request-indexing@1x.png" srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-request-indexing@1x.png, /media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-request-indexing@2x.png 2x" title="Screenshot of Google Search Console’s Request Indexing feature" width="600"/></picture></div>
<p>To the right of <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">View Crawled Page</code>, you’ll find the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Request Indexing</code> feature, allowing you get a URL indexed quickly (sometimes even within minutes). That doesn’t necessarily mean this URL will <em>rank</em> for any queries though. It just means the URL will come up for <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">site:</code> queries and such.</p>
<p>In general, it does decrease the time it takes for Google to actually rank the page for queries.</p>
<p>Please note that the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Request Indexing</code> feature doesn't replace <a href="/academy/google-index/">the indexing best practices</a>, and that there's <a href="https://nickleroy.com/blog-posts/request-indexing-gsc-limit/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="extlink">a limit of 10-12 request per day</span><span class="show-for-sr"> (opens in a new tab)</span></a>.</p>

<h3 class="toc" id="coverage">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#coverage" tabindex="-1"></a>4. Coverage</h3>
<p>The first thing you’ll see in the Coverage section is the “main coverage” status of the URL—for example the familiar <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Submitted and Indexed</code> in the screenshot below. These statuses are also used in the <a href="/academy/index-coverage/">Index Coverage report</a>.</p>
<p>When you unfold the Coverage section, it shows you how Google fared in the three phases it went through for this URL:</p>
<ol>
<li><code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Discovery</code></li>
<li><code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Crawl</code></li>
<li><code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Indexing</code></li>
</ol>
<div class="media-container" style="max-width: 600px;"><picture><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-view-coverage-pane@1x.avif" type="image/avif"/><source srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-view-coverage-pane@1x.avif, /media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-view-coverage-pane@2x.avif 2x" type="image/avif"/><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-view-coverage-pane@1x.webp" type="image/webp"/><source srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-view-coverage-pane@1x.webp, /media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-view-coverage-pane@2x.webp 2x" type="image/webp"/><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-view-coverage-pane@1x.png"/><img alt="Screenshot of Google Search Console’s Coverage section" class="image" height="445" loading="lazy" src="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-view-coverage-pane@1x.png" srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-view-coverage-pane@1x.png, /media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-interface-view-coverage-pane@2x.png 2x" title="Screenshot of Google Search Console’s Coverage section" width="600"/></picture></div>
<h4 id="coverage-discovery">Discovery</h4>
<p>The Discovery section explains how Google discovered the URL, listing the following fields:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<strong>Sitemaps:</strong> lists the submitted XML sitemap(s) the URL is included in. If the URL isn’t included in an XML sitemap, your robots.txt doesn’t specify your XML sitemaps, and/or you haven’t submitted a relevant XML sitemap in Google Search Console, it will just say <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">N/A</code>.</li>
<li>
<strong>Referring page</strong>: lists possible candidates through which Google may have learned about the URL, limited to a maximum of four URLs shown. This could list pages that don't link to the URL you’re inspecting. In that case, it’s showing a “proxy page” through which Google learned about your URL.</li>
</ul>
<p>There doesn’t seem to be any logic behind the (order of) URLs that’s shown there. You’d expect the most relevant and authoritative URLs to be featured, but that’s not necessarily the case. URLs shown here may be from your own site or other websites, or may even be a redirect.</p>
<p>If no URL is known, it will say <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">None detected</code>. In case the referring URL isn’t available to Google Search Console for some reason, it will additionally show “URL might be known from other sources that are currently not reported.” We’ve seen this to happen when a URL was discovered through social media posts that are no longer available.</p>
<h4 id="coverage-crawl">Crawl</h4>
<p>The Crawl section describes how Google fared the last time it crawled the page. It lists the following fields:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<strong>Last crawl</strong>: shows the date and time this URL was last crawled, in your local time. If the URL hasn’t been crawled yet, it will show <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">N/A</code>.</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Crawled as</strong>: shows the user-agent Google used when last crawling the URL. In most cases, this will show <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Googlebot smartphone</code>, because Google’s moving to a world in which mobile-first indexing is the standard. However, if your site hasn’t yet moved over to mobile-first crawls, it will show <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Googlebot desktop</code>. If the URL hasn’t been crawled yet, it will show <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">N/A</code>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<strong>Crawl allowed?</strong>: shows whether Google was allowed to crawl the URL. Possible values are:
    <ul>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Yes</code>: Google was allowed to crawl it.</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">No: blocked by robots.txt</code>—Google couldn’t crawl the URL because of robots.txt restrictions.</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">N/A</code>: Google hasn’t crawled the URL yet, because it’s still in the <a href="/academy/index-coverage/faq/discovered-not-indexed/">Discovered - currently not indexed</a> status, or won’t crawl it because it’s a non-existing URL… one that returns HTTP status 404, for instance.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<strong>Page fetch</strong>: describes whether or not Google was able to fetch the page, and why. If crawling is disallowed, Google will not be able to fetch the page. Possible values are:
    <ul>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Successful</code>—Google fetched the page successfully.</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Failed: Soft 404</code>—Google considers the page to be a <a href="/academy/index-coverage/faq/submitted-soft-404/">soft 404</a>.</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Failed: Not found (404)</code>—The page returned HTTP status 404 to Google.</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Failed: Crawl anomaly</code>—Google encountered a <a href="/academy/index-coverage/faq/crawl-anomaly/">crawl anomaly</a>, meaning it had issues fetching the page. For example, it may have returned HTTP status code 404.</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Failed: Blocked by robots.txt</code>—Google couldn’t fetch this URL because it was blocked by robots.txt. This status is returned for two Index Coverage report statuses: <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Blocked by robots.txt</code> and <a href="/academy/index-coverage/faq/indexed-blocked/">Indexed, though blocked by robots.txt</a>.</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Failed: Redirect error</code>—Google could not follow the redirect for some reason. This could be a <a href="/academy/redirects/faq/redirect-chains/">redirect chain</a> or a <a href="/academy/redirects/faq/redirect-loops/">redirect loop</a>.</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">N/A</code>—Google couldn’t determine or hasn’t yet determined whether or not to index the URL.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<strong>Indexing allowed?</strong>: describes whether or not Google is allowed to index the URL. Possible values are:
    <ul>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Yes</code>—Google is allowed to index the URL. Please note that Google will also show <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Yes</code> here in cases where Google had already indexed the URL before it was blocked by robots.txt.</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">No</code>—Google isn’t allowed to index the URL, for instance because the <a href="/academy/index-coverage/faq/submitted-noindex/">URL may be marked noindex</a>.</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">N/A</code>—for instance when Google has dismissed the URL and won’t even check whether it is allowed to index it. This value is shown for the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Failed: Soft 404</code> page fetch status.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>

<h4 id="coverage-indexing">Indexing</h4>
<p>The Indexing section describes the canonical URL Google found for the page, as well as the outcome of its indexing process. It lists the following fields:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<strong>User-declared canonical</strong>: shows whether you’ve defined a canonical URL for the page, and if so, what it is. Possible values are:
    <ul>
<li>The URL you defined as the canonical URL.</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">None</code>—you haven’t defined any canonical URL. URLs with no canonical URL defined often have the status <a href="/academy/index-coverage/faq/duplicate-without-canonical/">Duplicate without user-selected canonical</a>.</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">N/A</code>—in cases where Google couldn’t fetch the page. For example, you’ll find this value when the URL has the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Discovered - currently not indexed</code> or <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Crawled - currently not indexed</code> statuses.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<strong>Google-selected canonical</strong>: shows which canonical Google found to be most suitable. Possible values are:
    <ul>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Inspected URL</code>—Google chose the URL you’re inspecting as the canonical for this URL.</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Same as user-declared canonical</code>—When you see this, the canonical URL isn’t self-referencing, but instead points to another URL. Google has accepted the canonical URL you defined.</li>
<li>A different URL that Google considered more fitting than the canonical you defined yourself. When this happens, Google just ignores your canonical and applies the one it decided on itself.</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Only determined after indexing</code>—Google hasn’t indexed the page yet, so it hasn’t given a verdict yet.</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">URL cannot be displayed because it’s not in any of your properties</code>—Google can’t provide information here because the URL shown isn’t in any of your verified properties. If you want to find out the canonical URL Google has selected for your URL, run a <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">site:</code> for your own URL.</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">N/A</code>—for the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Discovered - currently not indexed</code> status, for example.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="toc" id="enhancements">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#enhancements" tabindex="-1"></a>5. Enhancements</h3>
<p>Under the “Coverage” section, you’ll find the Enhancements section:</p>

<p>Here, Google reports whether it encountered any issues in relation to AMP, Mobile Usability, and your Schema implementation, and whether you need to make improvements.</p>
<p>When a URL hasn’t been indexed yet, you’ll find the message “Only indexed URLs can have enhancements”. Once the URL has been indexed by Google, it’ll update automatically with their findings.</p>
<h4 id="mobile-usability">Mobile Usability</h4>
<p>In the screenshot above, it says <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Page is mobile friendly</code>, meaning everything looks good, and there’s nothing to worry about.</p>
<p>If Google did encounter Mobile Usability issues, they’ll be listed here. Other statuses are:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Page is not mobile friendly</code>: Google found issues that you need to address in order to provide your visitors with a mobile-friendly experience.</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">No data available</code>: Google wasn’t able to fetch the page, or it was, but it had issues in testing its mobile-friendliness. It’s best to try again later.</li>
</ul>
<p>Examples of issues Google may flag are:</p>
<ul>
<li>Text too small to read</li>
<li>Clickable elements too close together</li>
</ul>
<p>Speaking from experience, we often see false positives for Mobile Usability issues. So we always indicate the issue has been fixed and request verification. In most cases, the issues go away. It could have been that some of your assets (JavaScript or CSS files) were temporarily unavailable, causing the page to render incorrectly.</p>
<section aria-label="Pro tip" class="highlighted-box typo-block highlighted-box--with-icon highlighted-box--protip-style"><div aria-hidden="true" class="highlighted-box__title">Pro tip</div>
<div class="highlighted-box__content">
<p>Even though <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Core Web Vitals</code> are categorized under <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Enhancements</code> in the sidebar, they’re not reported on (yet) at the URL level. Therefore, you won’t find these reports when using the URL Inspection Tool.</p>
</div></section>
<h4 id="schema">Schema</h4>
<p>If Google <a href="https://support.google.com/webmasters/answer/9012289#enhancements" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="extlink">doesn’t yet support</span><span class="show-for-sr"> (opens in a new tab)</span></a> your implemented Schema types, or couldn’t find any, they’ll return “URL has no enhancements.”</p>
<p>If they do find and support your implemented Schema, they’ll report whether there’s anything that you can and/or should improve.</p>
<p>Common enhancements you’ll see are:</p>
<ul>
<li>Bread crumbs</li>
<li>Event</li>
<li>FAQPage</li>
<li>Job Postings</li>
<li>Logos</li>
<li>Product</li>
<li>Recipe</li>
<li>Review snippets</li>
</ul>
<p>If you recognize this feedback, that’s not a coincidence, as Google provides the exact same feedback here as what they provide through their <a href="https://search.google.com/test/rich-results" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="extlink">Rich Results Test</span><span class="show-for-sr"> (opens in a new tab)</span></a>.</p>

<h3 class="toc" id="test-live-url">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#test-live-url" tabindex="-1"></a>6. Test Live URL</h3>
<p>After you’ve pushed fixes live or made changes, you want to verify that they are correct.</p>
<p>The URL Inspection Tool shows you what information Google <em>has on file</em> about your URL, but that’s likely to be outdated—especially if you’ve just made changes. That’s where the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Test Live URL</code> feature comes in; it lets you run a live test on a URL to see what Google thinks of the URL’s <em>current</em> state.</p>
<p>Before diving into the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Test Live URL</code> feature, here’s what you should know to interpret its results correctly.</p>
<p>The live test…</p>
<ul>
<li>Doesn’t take into account manual actions and removal requests.</li>
<li>Doesn’t check the presence of the URL in your sitemaps, or whether there are referring pages.</li>
<li>Will show different statuses for “Presence on Google” and “Coverage” than you’re used to, because Google doesn’t go through the whole indexing process live.</li>
</ul>
<p>Let’s jump in without further ado!</p>
<p>On the right of the screen, you’ll find the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Test Live URL</code> button:</p>

<p>Hit the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Test Live URL</code> button to see what Google’s new verdict is. A box pops up with a loading screen. After a minute or so, you’ll see the live test results, starting with the “Presence on Google” status.</p>

<p>Please note that when you submit redirecting URLs to the live test, you’ll find the results of the redirect target, rather than the redirect source.</p>

<h4 id="test-live-url-availabile">Available to Google</h4>
<p>These presence statuses are slightly different from what we saw in the <a href="/academy/url-inspection-tool/#presence-on-google">URL presence statuses section</a> above. For instance, the “URL is on Google” and “URL is on Google, but has issues” statuses aren’t used here, because the live URL test is looking at whether it’s <em>possible</em> for Google to crawl and index the URL you’re testing.</p>
<p>Possible statuses you’ll see:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/academy/url-inspection-tool/#test-live-url-available">URL is available to Google</a></li>
<li><a href="/academy/url-inspection-tool/#test-live-url-available-but-issues">URL is available to Google, but has issues</a></li>
<li><a href="/academy/url-inspection-tool/#test-live-url-not-available">URL is not available to Google</a></li>
<li><a href="/academy/url-inspection-tool/#test-live-url-alternate-version">URL is an alternate version</a></li>
</ul>
<h5 id="test-live-url-available">URL is available to Google</h5>
<p>This status is accompanied by the following explanation:</p>
<p>"<em>If it gets indexed and selected as canonical, it could appear in Google Search results with all relevant enhancements.</em>"</p>
<p><strong>What this means</strong></p>
<p>Google can crawl the URL, and if it’s considered to be the canonical URL, it can appear in Google Search with all of its relevant enhancements.</p>
<p>Keep in mind that Google doesn’t take manual actions and removal requests into account here.</p>
<section aria-label="Action required" class="highlighted-box typo-block highlighted-box--with-icon highlighted-box--action-style"><div aria-hidden="true" class="highlighted-box__title">Action required</div>
<div class="highlighted-box__content">
<p>With this status, usually no action is required.</p>
<p>If you’re finding the URL isn’t ranking: tweak the content, add more internal links, and gain backlinks to improve its performance.</p>
</div></section>
<h5 id="test-live-url-available-but-issues">URL is available to Google, but has issues</h5>
<p>This status is accompanied by the following explanation:</p>
<p>"<em>If it gets indexed and selected as canonical, it could appear in google search results. However, some issues prevent it from being eligible for all enhancements.</em>"</p>
<p><strong>What this means</strong></p>
<p>Google can crawl the URL, and if it’s considered to be the canonical URL, it can appear in Google Search. Its enhancements may only appear partially, or not at all.</p>
<section aria-label="Action required" class="highlighted-box typo-block highlighted-box--with-icon highlighted-box--action-style"><div aria-hidden="true" class="highlighted-box__title">Action required</div>
<div class="highlighted-box__content">
<p>Investigate what issues Google has found with the URL’s enhancements and fix these if needed. If they are just warnings that actually don’t impact your enhancements’ visibility, you can leave them be. Just make sure you’re happy with how Google surfaces your URL.</p>
<p>If you do need to fix something, again use the live test to verify your fixes were correct.</p>
</div></section>
<h5 id="test-live-url-not-available">URL is not available to Google</h5>
<p>This status is accompanied by the following brief explanation:</p>
<p>"<em>It cannot be indexed.</em>"</p>
<p><strong>What this means</strong></p>
<p>Google can’t crawl the URL, which means they won’t be able to index it either. Oftentimes, this happens when Google can’t access a URL because it’s blocked by robots.txt, it’s behind HTTP authentication, or it returns a <a href="/academy/http-status-codes/#4xx">4xx</a> or <a href="/academy/http-status-codes/faq/server-error-5xx/">5xx status code</a>.</p>
<section aria-label="Action required" class="highlighted-box typo-block highlighted-box--with-icon highlighted-box--action-style"><div aria-hidden="true" class="highlighted-box__title">Action required</div>
<div class="highlighted-box__content">
<p>Assuming you want this URL to rank, investigate what’s preventing Google from crawling and indexing it. The information in the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Coverage</code> page will be helpful here. Fix any issues and double-check that nothing is keeping Google from crawling and indexing the page.</p>
<p>Then retry the live test to check if the issue has been resolved.</p>
</div></section>
<h5 id="test-live-url-alternate-version">URL is an alternate version</h5>
<p>This presence is exactly the same as what we described in the <a href="/academy/url-inspection-tool/#url-is-an-alternate-version">URL is an alternate version</a> section.</p>

<h4 id="availability">Availability</h4>
<p>Under <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Available to Google</code>, you’ll find the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Availability</code> section, which you can expand similarly to the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Coverage</code> section we covered earlier.</p>
<div class="media-container" style="max-width: 600px;"><picture><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-live-test-availability-pane@1x.avif" type="image/avif"/><source srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-live-test-availability-pane@1x.avif, /media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-live-test-availability-pane@2x.avif 2x" type="image/avif"/><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-live-test-availability-pane@1x.webp" type="image/webp"/><source srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-live-test-availability-pane@1x.webp, /media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-live-test-availability-pane@2x.webp 2x" type="image/webp"/><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-live-test-availability-pane@1x.png"/><img alt="Screenshot of the live-tested page’s Availability section" class="image" height="458" loading="lazy" src="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-live-test-availability-pane@1x.png" srcset="/media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-live-test-availability-pane@1x.png, /media/content/contentking-gsc-url-inspection-tool-live-test-availability-pane@2x.png 2x" title="Screenshot of the live-tested page’s Availability section" width="600"/></picture></div>
<p><code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Availability</code> shows fewer statuses than <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Coverage</code>, because the live test doesn’t take into account XML sitemaps and whether or not pages are indexed.</p>
<p>Possible values are:</p>
<ul>
<li><code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">URL can be indexed</code></li>
<li><code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Blocked by robots.txt</code></li>
<li><code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Excluded by ‘noindex’ tag</code></li>
<li><code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Redirect error</code></li>
<li><code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Soft 404</code></li>
<li><code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Not found (404)</code></li>
</ul>
<p>The Availability section is split up into three sub-sections:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<strong>Discovery</strong>—this isn’t covered by the live test because you’re testing URLs in isolation. Therefore this will always say “Not checked in live tests.”</li>
<li>
<strong>Crawl</strong>—this covers the exact same fields as the ones described earlier in the <a href="/academy/url-inspection-tool/#coverage">Coverage section</a>.</li>
<li>
<strong>Indexing</strong>—shows the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">User-declared canonical</code>, but the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Google-selected canonical</code> will always be <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Only determined after indexing</code>, because the URL would need to go through the full indexing and canonicalization process, which can’t be done when you’re testing URLs in isolation. For instance, the canonicalization process also takes into account incoming internal and external links, canonical URLs from other pages and XML sitemap signals.</li>
</ul>
<h4 id="test-live-url-enhancements">Enhancements</h4>
<p>The kind of information Google reports in the <a href="/academy/url-inspection-tool/#enhancements">Enhancements section</a> when testing live URLs is the same as what we described in the previous chapter. The only difference with the live URL test is that you receive feedback about the <em>current</em> version of the URL.</p>
<h2 id="faqs-url-inspection-tool">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#faqs-url-inspection-tool" tabindex="-1"></a>Frequently asked questions</h2>
<h3 id="-why-is-my-url-not-ranking-even-though-the-url-inspection-tool-says-url-is-on-google">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#-why-is-my-url-not-ranking-even-though-the-url-inspection-tool-says-url-is-on-google" tabindex="-1"></a>🤔 Why is my URL not ranking, even though the URL Inspection Tool says “URL is on Google”?</h3>
<p>The fact that your URL is <em>indexed _by Google doesn’t necessarily mean it’s _ranking</em>. For a URL to rank, it often takes more than just the fact that Google was able to index the URL.</p>
<p>To name a few factors: the page should have high-quality content that satisfies a user’s intent. It needs internal links, and it needs links from other websites.</p>
<h3 id="-why-does-the-url-inspection-tool-show-an-old-state-of-my-url">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#-why-does-the-url-inspection-tool-show-an-old-state-of-my-url" tabindex="-1"></a>🧾 Why does the URL Inspection Tool show an old state of my URL?</h3>
<p>Google hasn’t come around yet to recrawling and reindexing your URL. To speed up this process, you can do the following:</p>
<ol>
<li>Make sure the URL is included in your XML sitemap.</li>
<li>Add more internal links to the URL from high-authority pages on your site.</li>
<li>Acquire links from other websites to the URL.</li>
<li>Include your URL in a post on Google My Business.</li>
</ol>
<h3 id="-does-the-url-inspection-tool-account-for-manual-actions">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#-does-the-url-inspection-tool-account-for-manual-actions" tabindex="-1"></a>🧐 Does the URL Inspection Tool account for manual actions?</h3>
<p>No, it doesn’t. So keep that in mind when diagnosing indexing and ranking issues. In case of doubt, be sure to check Google Search Console’s <a href="https://search.google.com/search-console/manual-actions" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="extlink">Manual Actions section</span><span class="show-for-sr"> (opens in a new tab)</span></a> to double-check that nothing is shown there.</p>
<p>On top of that, the URL inspection Tool also doesn’t take <a href="https://www.google.com/webmasters/tools/removals" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="extlink">Removal Requests</span><span class="show-for-sr"> (opens in a new tab)</span></a> into account.</p>

<p>When you use our website <a href="https://www.contentkingapp.com/">www.contentkingapp.com</a> (“Website”), we may place cookies. This Cookie Policy explains what cookies are and in which way cookies are stored on, and information is read from, your computer, mobile device and/or tablet (“Devices”). Please read this Cookie Policy carefully in order to understand what type of cookies ContentKing uses, the information we collect using cookies and how that information is used.</p>
<h2 id="what-are-cookies">What are cookies?</h2>
<p>Cookies are small text files which are stored on the browser or hard drive of your Device when you visit a webpage or use an application. Cookies may be needed to show the webpage or application on your Device and are also used to enhance the user experience. Cookies cannot damage your Devices or the files saved on it. There are different types of cookies. Some cookies come directly from our website (first party cookies) and others come from third parties which place cookies on our site (third party cookies).</p>
<p>Cookies can be stored for varying lengths of time on your browser or device. Session cookies are deleted from your computer or device when you close your web-browser. Persistent cookies will remain stored on your computer or Device until deleted or until they reach their expiry date.</p>
<h2 id="how-do-we-use-cookies">How do we use cookies?</h2>
<h3 id="essential-technical-cookies">Essential (Technical) cookies</h3>
<p>We try to provide an advanced and user-friendly website that adapts automatically to needs and wishes of our visitors and users. To achieve this, we use technical cookies to i.e. show you our Website, to make it function correctly, to create your ContentKing account, to sign you in and to manage your requests. These technical cookies are necessary for our website to function properly.</p>
<h3 id="functional-cookies">Functional cookies</h3>
<p>We also use functional cookies to remember your preferences and settings (as username, password, language etc.) and to help you to use the Website efficiently and effectively. These functional cookies are not strictly necessary for the functioning of our Website, but they add functionality for you and enhance your experience.</p>
<h3 id="analytical-cookies">Analytical cookies</h3>
<p>We use analytical cookies in order to collect statistics about the use and visits of the Website. These analytic cookies generate statistical and other information about website use by means of cookies, which are stored on users’ Devices. The information generated relating to our Website is used to create reports about the use of the Website. Analytical cookies may be placed without your consent, unless such cookies have an impact on your privacy. In such cases, prior consent will be asked for.</p>
<h3 id="google-analytics">Google Analytics</h3>
<p>We use Google Analytics cookies in order to collect statistics about the use and visits of the Website. With these analytical cookies a permanent cookie is saved on your device to register your use of the Website. Google Analytics analyses this data and provides us with the results. This way, we obtain insight into the traffic of the Website and the way in which the Website is used. Based on this information we are able to make specific adjustments to the Website.</p>
<p>The information that we obtain is transferred to Google and stored by Google on servers outside the European Economic Area. We have entered into a data processing agreement with Google in which agreements have been made regarding the processing of personal data.</p>
<p>If you would like more information about analytical cookies and their expiry date, please visit the Google Analytics <a href="https://developers.google.com/analytics/devguides/collection/gajs/cookie-usage">information page</a>. Google also offers an <a href="https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout?hl=en">opt-out option</a> for data collection within the scope of Google Analytics.</p>
<h3 id="marketing-cookies">Marketing cookies</h3>
<p>We use third party marketing cookies in order to follow your internet browsing behavior and collect data and information on your browsing behavior from various websites you visited. This information is used to make the content of the displayed advertisements as relevant as possible, and to limit repetition of the same advertisements.</p>
<h3 id="social-plug-ins">Social plug-ins</h3>
<p>By using the Website you can get access to social media websites, such as Facebook, Twitter and LinkedIn. These buttons come with a code which has been made by these networks themselves. Using these so called social plug-ins you can login to your social media profile and subsequently share information from the Website with others. With these social plug-ins third party marketing cookies are saved on your Devices. These cookies serve the purpose of optimizing your user experience.</p>
<p>Companies such as LinkedIn, Twitter and Facebook may share your personal data outside the European Economic Area. Please read our Privacy Policy <a href="/legal/privacy-policy/">here</a> and the respective social network’s privacy statement to see how these companies treat your (personal) data.</p>
<h3 id="pixels">Pixels</h3>
<p>We also place pixels of third parties. A pixel keeps track of your surfing behavior and how you use the Website. These data are aggregated and give us information about the target group that visits our website. Based on this information, we can show an advertisement to a specific or similar target group on the website of the third party that produced the pixel.</p>
<h2 id="disable-or-delete-cookies">Disable or delete cookies</h2>
<p>When you first visit the Website, we ask you to give consent for cookies that are not necessary or functional and that have an impact on your privacy. If you do not give consent, these cookies will not be placed. In that case, you may not be able to use all the functionalities on our Website.</p>
<p>You can configure your browser so that you do not receive any cookies the next time you use the website. You can read here how you can remove different types of cookies in different browsers. Please consult the help-function of your browser if your browser is not listed below.</p>
