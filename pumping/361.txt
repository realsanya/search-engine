<h1 class="heading__title">Change Tracking on Issues</h1>
</div>
<div class="post-detail__text typo">

<div aria-hidden="true" class="highlighted-box__title">Table of contents</div>

<p>As ContentKing is monitoring 24/7, your website is constantly being audited for SEO issues, providing you with real-time data about the problems that prevent your website from reaching its full SEO potential.</p>
<p>The issues that ContentKing detects on your website determine the Website Health score, which takes into account how many pages are affected by the individual issues as well as how important the affected pages are within the website.</p>
<h2 id="change-tracking-on-issues">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#change-tracking-on-issues" tabindex="-1"></a>Change Tracking on Issues</h2>
<p>Apart from providing you with real-time data about the SEO issues that are being detected on the website, ContentKing also tracks changes of all of the reported issues historically, enabling you to see how the issues changed in a selected date range.</p>
<p>This means that you can easily keep track of issues being solved, issues being introduced, or spot regressions between releases. Whether you want to view issue changes for the past few days or the past half a year, you can just select the date range you need and dig in.</p>
<p>Also, if you see your <em>Website Health</em> score drop or increase and you want to learn why, Change Tracking on Issues will provide you with this information. The feature allows you to see how exactly each issue change impacted the <em>Health</em> score.</p>
<h2 id="how-to-access-change-tracking-on-issues">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#how-to-access-change-tracking-on-issues" tabindex="-1"></a>How to access Change Tracking on Issues</h2>
<p>There are multiple places in the app from which you can access the Change Tracking on Issues feature:</p>
<ul>
<li>The Issues screen</li>
<li>Weekly Report that you receive via email</li>
<li>The Dashboard screen</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="access-from-the-weekly-report">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#access-from-the-weekly-report" tabindex="-1"></a>Access from the Weekly Report</h3>
<p>Every Monday ContentKing sends you the weekly email report that keeps you up-to-date with the development of key ContentKing metrics for all of your websites.</p>
<p>In the report, you can see a chart showing how many issues were opened and resolved on the website as well as in specific segments, and also how the <em>Website/Segment Health</em> score changed.</p>
<p>When you click one of the charts, you will get to the Issue Change Tracking screen where you will see exactly which issues were opened and resolved during the past week and how these changes impacted the <em>Health</em> score.</p>

<h3 id="access-from-the-dashboard-screen">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#access-from-the-dashboard-screen" tabindex="-1"></a>Access from the Dashboard screen</h3>
<p>The <em>Website Health</em> chart on the Dashboard in ContentKing shows you the daily or weekly development of the website’s <em>Health</em> score up to 6 weeks back.</p>
<p>If you see a drop or gain in the <em>Website Health</em> and you want to find out what caused it, click the point in the chart and ContentKing will take you to the Issue Change Tracking screen with all issue changes that affected the <em>Health</em> score within that day or week.</p>

<h3 id="access-from-the-issues-screen">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#access-from-the-issues-screen" tabindex="-1"></a>Access from the Issues screen</h3>
<p>When you are in Issues, you can access the change tracking feature by clicking the <em>Tracked Changes</em> button in the top-right corner of the screen, next to the <em>Website Health</em> bar.</p>

<h2 id="setting-a-date-range">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#setting-a-date-range" tabindex="-1"></a>Setting a date range</h2>
<p>After you access Tracked Changes in Issues, you can select the date range for which you want to view the changes.</p>
<p>In case of accessing the feature from the Weekly report or the Dashboard, the date range is already pre-set based on the data you clicked in the chart. However, you can also set a custom date range according to your needs.</p>
<p>To set or change the date range, click the <em>Change Date Range</em> button in the top-right corner of the screen. You will see the following panel with a calendar:</p>
<div class="media-container" style="max-width: 600px;"><picture><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-setting-date-range@1x.avif" type="image/avif"/><source srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-setting-date-range@1x.avif, /media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-setting-date-range@2x.avif 2x" type="image/avif"/><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-setting-date-range@1x.webp" type="image/webp"/><source srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-setting-date-range@1x.webp, /media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-setting-date-range@2x.webp 2x" type="image/webp"/><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-setting-date-range@1x.png"/><img alt="Screenshot of a calendar that allows the user to set a date range for the issue changes in ContentKing" class="image" height="356" loading="lazy" src="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-setting-date-range@1x.png" srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-setting-date-range@1x.png, /media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-setting-date-range@2x.png 2x" title="Setting a date range for the issue changes in ContentKing" width="600"/></picture></div>
<p>On the right side of the panel, you can select a predefined date range. You can choose:</p>
<ul>
<li>Today</li>
<li>Last 7 days</li>
<li>Last 2 weeks</li>
<li>Last 4 weeks</li>
</ul>
<p>Alternatively, you can also set a custom date range: just select the start date and the end date in the calendar. For the past 8 days, you can always select daily intervals. If you want to go further back than the past 8 days, you can only track changes in weekly intervals.</p>

<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#charts" tabindex="-1"></a>Charts</h2>

<ul>
<li><em>Health</em></li>
<li><em>Issues</em></li>
</ul>
<p>The <em>Health</em> chart shows the development of the <em>Website/Segment Health</em> score in the selected date range and on the left side of the charts you can see how many <em>Health</em> score points the website gained or lost during that time period.</p>

<h2 id="types-of-tracked-changes">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#types-of-tracked-changes" tabindex="-1"></a>Types of tracked changes</h2>
<p>There are several types of issue changes that can have an impact on the <em>Website Health</em> or the <em>Segment Health</em>.</p>
<p>When you click on one of the issue categories on the left side of the screen, you will see a breakdown of the different types of changes within the issue category that impacted the <em>Health</em> score.</p>
<p>First you will always see the primary issue changes that are the most important in relation to the <em>Website Health</em> or the <em>Segment Health</em>:</p>
<p><strong>Issue opened</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Issue opened on existing pages</code>
<ul>
<li>An existing page became affected by the issue</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Issue opened on added pages</code>
<ul>
<li>ContentKing discovered a new page that was affected by the issue</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Issue resolved</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Issue resolved</code></li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Issue ignoring</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Issue ignored</code></li>
<li><code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Issue unignored</code></li>
</ul>

<p>However, there are also other types of changes that impact the <em>Health</em> score of the website or segment. You can view these changes by clicking the <em>Show other changes impacting Website/Segment Health button</em>.</p>
<p>These changes are:</p>
<p><strong>Segment changed</strong></p>
<p>This change type applies when you’re tracking issue changes in a specific segment. The segments in ContentKing are dynamic, which means that they always include pages that are matching the segment criteria at the moment.</p>
<p>As soon as a page starts matching the segment criteria, it enters the segment. On the other hand, when a page stops matching the segment criteria, it leaves the segment (more about this <a href="/support/segments/">here</a>.</p>
<p>The issue-related segment changes include the following:</p>
<ul>
<li><code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Affected pages entered the segment</code></li>
<li><code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Affected pages left the segment</code></li>
<li><code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Unaffected pages entered the segment</code></li>
<li><code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Unaffected pages left the segment</code></li>
</ul>
<div class="media-container" style="max-width: 600px;"><picture><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-segment-changed@1x.avif" type="image/avif"/><source srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-segment-changed@1x.avif, /media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-segment-changed@2x.avif 2x" type="image/avif"/><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-segment-changed@1x.webp" type="image/webp"/><source srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-segment-changed@1x.webp, /media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-segment-changed@2x.webp 2x" type="image/webp"/><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-segment-changed@1x.png"/><img alt="Screenshot of the Segment changes that have an impact on the Health score" class="image" height="494" loading="lazy" src="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-segment-changed@1x.png" srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-segment-changed@1x.png, /media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-segment-changed@2x.png 2x" title="The Segment changes that have an impact on the Health score" width="600"/></picture></div>
<p><strong>No longer applicable</strong></p>
<p>These are changes of affected pages that result in the issue not being applicable on the page anymore due to its nature:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Issue became not applicable</code>
<ul>
<li>Example: the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Page title is too long</code> issue stops being applicable when the page title is removed from the page.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Pages became non-pages</code>
<ul>
<li>Example: the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Page title is missing</code> issue stops being applicable when the page turns into a redirect.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<div class="media-container" style="max-width: 600px;"><picture><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-no-longer-applicable@1x.avif" type="image/avif"/><source srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-no-longer-applicable@1x.avif, /media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-no-longer-applicable@2x.avif 2x" type="image/avif"/><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-no-longer-applicable@1x.webp" type="image/webp"/><source srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-no-longer-applicable@1x.webp, /media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-no-longer-applicable@2x.webp 2x" type="image/webp"/><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-no-longer-applicable@1x.png"/><img alt="Screenshot of the issue changes in the No longer applicable category that have an impact on the Health score" class="image" height="692" loading="lazy" src="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-no-longer-applicable@1x.png" srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-no-longer-applicable@1x.png, /media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-no-longer-applicable@2x.png 2x" title="Issue changes in the No longer applicable category that have an impact on the Health score" width="600"/></picture></div>
<p><strong>Unaffected pages</strong></p>
<p>These are changes of pages that are not affected by the issue but still had an impact on the Health score:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Issue became applicable</code>
<ul>
<li>Example: a URL was a redirect, so the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Page title is missing</code> issue wasn’t applicable. However, the URL became a page and has a page title now, so the issue is applicable on the page now but the page is not affected by the issue (as the page title is present).</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Issue became not applicable</code>.
    <ul>
<li>Example: A page had a page title with the correct length. However, the page title was removed from the page, so the <em>Page title has a correct length</em> issue became not applicable.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Issue became required</code>
<ul>
<li>Example: the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Page title issues</code> were only required on indexable pages, but you changed the <a href="/support/issue-configuration/">Issue configuration</a> and now they are required on all pages (including the non-indexable pages).</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Issue became not required</code>
<ul>
<li>Example: the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Page title issues</code> were required on all pages, but you changed the <a href="/support/issue-configuration/">Issue configuration</a> and now they are only required on indexable pages and stopped being required on non-indexable pages.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Added pages</code>
<ul>
<li>Example: ContentKing discovered a new page that was added to the website within the selected date range and the page is not affected by the issue.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Pages became non-pages</code>
<ul>
<li>Example: An unaffected page changed type from <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Page</code> (2xx) to <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Redirect</code> (3xx), <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Missing</code> (4xx), <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Server Error</code> (5xx) or <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Unreachable</code>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<div class="media-container" style="max-width: 600px;"><picture><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-unaffected-pages@1x.avif" type="image/avif"/><source srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-unaffected-pages@1x.avif, /media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-unaffected-pages@2x.avif 2x" type="image/avif"/><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-unaffected-pages@1x.webp" type="image/webp"/><source srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-unaffected-pages@1x.webp, /media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-unaffected-pages@2x.webp 2x" type="image/webp"/><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-unaffected-pages@1x.png"/><img alt="Screenshot of the issue changes in the Unaffected pages category that have an impact on the Health score" class="image" height="554" loading="lazy" src="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-unaffected-pages@1x.png" srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-unaffected-pages@1x.png, /media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-unaffected-pages@2x.png 2x" title="Issue changes in the Unaffected pages category that have an impact on the Health score" width="600"/></picture></div>
<p><strong>Importance changed</strong></p>
<p>The more important a page is, the bigger impact – positive or negative – its issues have on the <em>Health</em> score. Therefore changes of pages’ <em>Importance</em> score also impact the <em>Health</em> score.</p>
<p>This includes the following change types:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Affected pages became less important</code>
<ul>
<li>Example: A page affected by the issue became less important, so the negative impact it has on the Health score became lower as well.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Affected pages became more important</code>
<ul>
<li>Example: A page affected by the issue became more important, so the negative impact it has on the Health score became stronger as well.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Unaffected pages became less important</code>
<ul>
<li>Example: A page that is not affected by the issue became more important, so the positive impact it has on the Health score became stronger as well.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Unaffected pages became more important</code>
<ul>
<li>Example: A page that is not affected by the issue became less important, so the positive impact it has on the Health score became lower as well.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<div class="media-container" style="max-width: 600px;"><picture><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-importance-changed@1x.avif" type="image/avif"/><source srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-importance-changed@1x.avif, /media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-importance-changed@2x.avif 2x" type="image/avif"/><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-importance-changed@1x.webp" type="image/webp"/><source srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-importance-changed@1x.webp, /media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-importance-changed@2x.webp 2x" type="image/webp"/><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-importance-changed@1x.png"/><img alt="Screenshot of the Importance score changes that have an impact on the Health score" class="image" height="483" loading="lazy" src="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-importance-changed@1x.png" srcset="/media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-importance-changed@1x.png, /media/support/contentking-change-tracking-issues-change-types-importance-changed@2x.png 2x" title="Changes of the Importance score of pages that have an impact on the Health score" width="600"/></picture></div>

<h2 id="what-are-cookies">What are cookies?</h2>
<p>Cookies are small text files which are stored on the browser or hard drive of your Device when you visit a webpage or use an application. Cookies may be needed to show the webpage or application on your Device and are also used to enhance the user experience. Cookies cannot damage your Devices or the files saved on it. There are different types of cookies. Some cookies come directly from our website (first party cookies) and others come from third parties which place cookies on our site (third party cookies).</p>
<p>Cookies can be stored for varying lengths of time on your browser or device. Session cookies are deleted from your computer or device when you close your web-browser. Persistent cookies will remain stored on your computer or Device until deleted or until they reach their expiry date.</p>
<h2 id="how-do-we-use-cookies">How do we use cookies?</h2>
<h3 id="essential-technical-cookies">Essential (Technical) cookies</h3>
<p>We try to provide an advanced and user-friendly website that adapts automatically to needs and wishes of our visitors and users. To achieve this, we use technical cookies to i.e. show you our Website, to make it function correctly, to create your ContentKing account, to sign you in and to manage your requests. These technical cookies are necessary for our website to function properly.</p>
<h3 id="functional-cookies">Functional cookies</h3>
<p>We also use functional cookies to remember your preferences and settings (as username, password, language etc.) and to help you to use the Website efficiently and effectively. These functional cookies are not strictly necessary for the functioning of our Website, but they add functionality for you and enhance your experience.</p>
<h3 id="analytical-cookies">Analytical cookies</h3>
<p>We use analytical cookies in order to collect statistics about the use and visits of the Website. These analytic cookies generate statistical and other information about website use by means of cookies, which are stored on users’ Devices. The information generated relating to our Website is used to create reports about the use of the Website. Analytical cookies may be placed without your consent, unless such cookies have an impact on your privacy. In such cases, prior consent will be asked for.</p>
<h3 id="google-analytics">Google Analytics</h3>
<p>We use Google Analytics cookies in order to collect statistics about the use and visits of the Website. With these analytical cookies a permanent cookie is saved on your device to register your use of the Website. Google Analytics analyses this data and provides us with the results. This way, we obtain insight into the traffic of the Website and the way in which the Website is used. Based on this information we are able to make specific adjustments to the Website.</p>
<p>The information that we obtain is transferred to Google and stored by Google on servers outside the European Economic Area. We have entered into a data processing agreement with Google in which agreements have been made regarding the processing of personal data.</p>
<p>If you would like more information about analytical cookies and their expiry date, please visit the Google Analytics <a href="https://developers.google.com/analytics/devguides/collection/gajs/cookie-usage">information page</a>. Google also offers an <a href="https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout?hl=en">opt-out option</a> for data collection within the scope of Google Analytics.</p>
<h3 id="marketing-cookies">Marketing cookies</h3>
<p>We use third party marketing cookies in order to follow your internet browsing behavior and collect data and information on your browsing behavior from various websites you visited. This information is used to make the content of the displayed advertisements as relevant as possible, and to limit repetition of the same advertisements.</p>
<h3 id="social-plug-ins">Social plug-ins</h3>
<p>By using the Website you can get access to social media websites, such as Facebook, Twitter and LinkedIn. These buttons come with a code which has been made by these networks themselves. Using these so called social plug-ins you can login to your social media profile and subsequently share information from the Website with others. With these social plug-ins third party marketing cookies are saved on your Devices. These cookies serve the purpose of optimizing your user experience.</p>
<p>Companies such as LinkedIn, Twitter and Facebook may share your personal data outside the European Economic Area. Please read our Privacy Policy <a href="/legal/privacy-policy/">here</a> and the respective social network’s privacy statement to see how these companies treat your (personal) data.</p>
<h3 id="pixels">Pixels</h3>
<p>We also place pixels of third parties. A pixel keeps track of your surfing behavior and how you use the Website. These data are aggregated and give us information about the target group that visits our website. Based on this information, we can show an advertisement to a specific or similar target group on the website of the third party that produced the pixel.</p>
<h2 id="disable-or-delete-cookies">Disable or delete cookies</h2>
<p>When you first visit the Website, we ask you to give consent for cookies that are not necessary or functional and that have an impact on your privacy. If you do not give consent, these cookies will not be placed. In that case, you may not be able to use all the functionalities on our Website.</p>
<p>You can configure your browser so that you do not receive any cookies the next time you use the website. You can read here how you can remove different types of cookies in different browsers. Please consult the help-function of your browser if your browser is not listed below.</p>
