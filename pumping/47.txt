<h1 class="heading__title">How to Handle Discontinued Products Without Ruining Your SEO</h1>
<div class="heading__params">
<span aria-hidden="true" class="heading__label">
										Last updated:
									</span>
<span aria-label="The date of last update" class="heading__caption">
										October 13, 2021
									</span>
</div>
</div>
<div class="post-detail__text typo">
<section aria-label="Discontinued product pages in short" class="highlighted-box highlighted-box--with-background typo-block"><div aria-hidden="true" class="highlighted-box__title">
<span class="highlighted-box__title-icon"><i class="post-type-icon post-type-icon--reversed-style" style="background-image: url(/assets/images/icons/post-types/discontinued-products--reversed.svg)"></i></span>Discontinued product pages in short
	</div>
<div class="highlighted-box__content">
<p>Discontinued product pages can have a huge effect on your SEO performance, and your sales. Whether you are dealing with products that are discontinued temporarily or permanently, you have to always balance:</p>
<ol>
<li>Crawl budget</li>
<li>SEO value</li>
<li>User experience.</li>
</ol>
<p>Here are SEO tips to avoid making the most common mistakes and to properly handle discontinued product pages.</p>
</div>
</section>
<div aria-label="Table of contents" class="highlighted-box" role="navigation">
<div aria-hidden="true" class="highlighted-box__title">Table of contents</div>

<li class="navigation-list__item navigation-list__item--secondary"><a href="#permanently-discontinued">How to handle permanently discontinued products</a></li>

<li class="navigation-list__item navigation-list__item--secondary"><a href="#preserve-crawl-budget">Preserving crawl budget</a></li>

</ul>
</div>
</div>
<p>We all know the eCommerce industry is highly competitive. When you mess up, your competitors benefit immediately. You need to be on top of your game and be the smartest person in the SERP.</p>
<p>One often overlooked aspect of eCommerce SEO is how to properly handle discontinued products. No matter whether these products are temporarily out of stock or permanently discontinued, their <a href="/academy/product-page-seo/">product pages</a> have a huge impact on your SEO performance.</p>
<p>Ignoring these implications can lead to losses of money and customers‚Äîand sometimes to tears and despair.</p>
<p>On the other hand, if handled properly, discontinued-product pages can even help you boost your rankings.</p>
<div class="media-container" style="max-width: 600px;"><picture><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/content/contentking-discontinued-products-comparison-en@1x.avif" type="image/avif"/><source srcset="/media/content/contentking-discontinued-products-comparison-en@1x.avif" type="image/avif"/><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/content/contentking-discontinued-products-comparison-en@1x.webp" type="image/webp"/><source srcset="/media/content/contentking-discontinued-products-comparison-en@1x.webp" type="image/webp"/><img alt="Illustration ‚Äî website owners with discontinued product pages" class="image" height="282" src="/media/content/contentking-discontinued-products-comparison-en@1x.png" title="Illustration ‚Äî website owners with discontinued product pages" width="600"/></picture></div>
<p>In this article, we‚Äôll describe the best practices for handling discontinued-product pages to retain what you‚Äôve worked so hard for, so that you can continue to increase your SEO visibility.</p>
<h2 id="what-is">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#what-is" tabindex="-1"></a>What is a discontinued product?</h2>
<p>First off, let‚Äôs define what a discontinued product is.</p>
<p>A discontinued product means a product that has become temporarily or permanently unavailable.</p>
<figure aria-labelledby="image-caption-2" class="captioned-image"><div class="media-container" style="max-width: 600px;"><picture><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/content/contentking-discontinued-pages-naked-palette-sold-out-page-en@1x.avif" type="image/avif"/><source srcset="/media/content/contentking-discontinued-pages-naked-palette-sold-out-page-en@1x.avif, /media/content/contentking-discontinued-pages-naked-palette-sold-out-page-en@2x.avif 2x" type="image/avif"/><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/content/contentking-discontinued-pages-naked-palette-sold-out-page-en@1x.webp" type="image/webp"/><source srcset="/media/content/contentking-discontinued-pages-naked-palette-sold-out-page-en@1x.webp, /media/content/contentking-discontinued-pages-naked-palette-sold-out-page-en@2x.webp 2x" type="image/webp"/><source media="(max-width: 600px)" srcset="/media/content/contentking-discontinued-pages-naked-palette-sold-out-page-en@1x.png"/><img alt="Screenshot - Naked Palette‚Äôs discontinued product page" class="image" height="356" loading="lazy" src="/media/content/contentking-discontinued-pages-naked-palette-sold-out-page-en@1x.png" srcset="/media/content/contentking-discontinued-pages-naked-palette-sold-out-page-en@1x.png, /media/content/contentking-discontinued-pages-naked-palette-sold-out-page-en@2x.png 2x" title="Screenshot - Naked Palette‚Äôs discontinued product page" width="600"/></picture></div>
<figcaption class="captioned-image__caption" id="image-caption-2">Naked Palette eyeliner was discontinued by Urban Decay in 2018. This is how its product page looks today.</figcaption></figure>
<p>Reasons for products being temporarily out of stock can include supply deficiencies, seasonality, or issues on the manufacturer side.</p>
<p>The permanent discontinuation of products, on the other hand, usually means that the product has been replaced by a new version or has proven unsuccessful, or its production has been simply stopped for good.</p>
<div aria-label="Embedded quote from our customer" class="quote-box typo-block">
<div aria-hidden="true" class="quote-box__avatar"><span class="avatar avatar--small-size"><span class="avatar__img"><picture><source srcset="/media/people/simon-cox@1x.avif, /media/people/simon-cox@2x.avif 2x" type="image/avif"/><source srcset="/media/people/simon-cox@1x.webp, /media/people/simon-cox@2x.webp 2x" type="image/webp"/><img alt="Simon Cox" class="image" height="200" loading="lazy" src="/media/people/simon-cox@1x.jpg" srcset="/media/people/simon-cox@1x.jpg, /media/people/simon-cox@2x.jpg 2x" title="Simon Cox" width="200"/></picture></span></span></div>
<div class="quote-box__content typo">
<blockquote class="quote-box__blockquote">
<p>With Out of Stock items we like to provide a date for expected new stock and a chance for the visitor to leave their email so we can contact them when its back in.</p>
<p>For discontinued items we like to provide an explanation, such as for example: "This item was discontinued on [date]" and a tailored search box to help the user's journey. If we can, we add similar items to choose from. If the item has been upgraded and has its own URL then place a link to it. We never 404 a URL unless there is absolutely no alternative.</p>
</blockquote>

<h2 id="keep-track">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#keep-track" tabindex="-1"></a>Keeping track of discontinued products</h2>
<p>Before you can even implement a strategy on how to handle discontinued products, you first need to know about the  products in question. How to get this data is different for every organization, perhaps you need to pull this data from an ERP system, CMS, Google Shopping feed or maybe you're using <a href="/blog/custom-element-extraction/">custom element monitoring</a>.</p>
<p>Do whatever you need to to get this data, because having a good discontinued products strategy in place can make or break your SEO performance.</p>
<div aria-label="Embedded quote from our customer" class="quote-box typo-block">
<div aria-hidden="true" class="quote-box__avatar"><span class="avatar avatar--small-size"><span class="avatar__img"><picture><source srcset="/media/people/luke-carthy@1x.avif, /media/people/luke-carthy@2x.avif 2x" type="image/avif"/><source srcset="/media/people/luke-carthy@1x.webp, /media/people/luke-carthy@2x.webp 2x" type="image/webp"/><img alt="Luke Carthy" class="image" height="100" loading="lazy" src="/media/people/luke-carthy@1x.jpg" srcset="/media/people/luke-carthy@1x.jpg, /media/people/luke-carthy@2x.jpg 2x" title="Luke Carthy" width="100"/></picture></span></span></div>
<div class="quote-box__content typo">
<blockquote class="quote-box__blockquote">
<p>Keeping tabs on all of your discontinued products across a living, breathing eCommerce site is tough. To make things easier and to give you a birds-eye view at any time, create a hit-scope custom dimension in Google Analytics called "product stock status" (i.e. "Current", "Out of stock" and "Discontinued").</p>
<p>Now, everytime a pageview is fired on a discontinued item, you pass the discontinued status to this custom dimension for that specific product.</p>
<p>You can then see all discontinued items with pageviews at any one time. Got huge demand for some lines? There could be an opportunity to bring it back (if you can) or invest time and resources in providing rock-solid alternatives and a class-leading CX for this specific item.</p>
<p>At the opposite end, if you've super-low levels of traffic for other discontinued items, you can make the necessary decisions for these lines too.</p>
<p>It's even more powerful and opportunistic if you leverage custom alerts and present this data in a Google Data Studio report too.</p>
</blockquote>

<h2 id="best-practices">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#best-practices" tabindex="-1"></a>Best practices for handling discontinued products</h2>
<p>The process of selecting the best strategy to get maximum SEO benefits out of your discontinued product pages will depend on whether the products will become available again in the future.</p>
<p>A temporarily discontinued product requires a different approach than a product that is simply no more. Let‚Äôs look at the two types separately.</p>
<h3 class="toc" id="temporarily-discontinued">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#temporarily-discontinued" tabindex="-1"></a>How to handle temporarily discontinued products</h3>
<p>If you expect your product to be available again, you should take care of retaining the URL‚Äôs SEO value and preparing your visitors for the product‚Äôs return. For a period of four to eight weeks, implement these quick measures:</p>
<ul>
<li>Keep the product alive and return the HTTP 200 OK status code</li>
<li>Keep the product in the <a href="/academy/xml-sitemap/">XML sitemap</a> and keep it in on-site search</li>
<li>Put ‚Äúout of stock‚Äù or a similar label on the product‚Äôs picture</li>
<li>Hide the product‚Äôs price and keep visitors from adding the item to the cart</li>
<li>Removing the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Offer</code> property from the Product Schema markup to prevent Schema penalties</li>
<li>Inform your visitors on when the product will be available again, or alternatively, offer them a way to receive a notification once it‚Äôs back in stock</li>
<li>Pause all active PPC campaigns directing customers to the product‚Äôs page</li>
</ul>
<section aria-label="Pro tip" class="highlighted-box typo-block highlighted-box--with-icon highlighted-box--protip-style"><div aria-hidden="true" class="highlighted-box__title">Pro tip</div>
<div class="highlighted-box__content">
<p><strong>Do not</strong> use ‚Äúout of stock,‚Äù ‚Äúitem removed‚Äù or similar phrases on the product‚Äôs detail page. Texts like these may make Google think of these pages as <a href="/academy/index-coverage/faq/submitted-soft-404/">soft-404 pages</a>, which can ruin a page‚Äôs rankings. That‚Äôs why you should specifically place this type of text in images.</p>
</div></section>
<p>After the waiting period is over and you expect the product to be back soon, you can extend the four-to-eight-weeks time as much as you prefer. But if there is no sign that the product will ever be available again, consider it discontinued permanently.</p>

<h3 class="toc" id="permanently-discontinued">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#permanently-discontinued" tabindex="-1"></a>How to handle permanently discontinued products</h3>
<p>If you‚Äôre sure that a product is discontinued for good, give the product the same treatment as you would give temporarily discontinued products.</p>
<p>Next, evaluate the page‚Äôs value by asking yourself these two questions:</p>
<ol>
<li>Are there any external links pointing to this product page?</li>
<li>Does this product page generate any traffic (be it organic, referral, social etc.)?</li>
</ol>
<h4 id="permanently-discontinued-no-seo-value">How to handle permanently discontinued products that have no value</h4>
<p>If the product page doesn‚Äôt have any value anymore, you can return <a href="/academy/http-status-codes/#410">HTTP status code 410</a>, which will signal to search engines that it no longer exists and show alternative products to visitors. Getting rid of the product page this way saves you precious <a href="/academy/crawl-budget/">crawl budget</a>, leaving room for search engines to crawl more meaningful parts of your website.</p>

<div class="highlighted-box__content">
<p>By using a 410 instead of a 404 you speed up the process of deindexing the page. The difference between a 404 and a 410 status code is that 404 means ‚ÄúNot Found‚Äù, while 410 stands for ‚ÄúGone‚Äù. The signal 410 ‚ÄúGone‚Äù sends is much stronger for search engines and makes it clear that you removed the URL on purpose. That‚Äôs why they‚Äôll deindex it faster.</p>
</div></section>
<h4 id="permanently-discontinued-seo-value">How to handle permanently discontinued products that do have value</h4>
<p>Returning a 410 for permanently discontinued products, that have many backlinks and still cater to an active audience, would be a huge mistake.</p>
<p>There's several approaches to making the most of this value, listed in order of preference:</p>
<ol>
<li>Keep alive as long as search demand is high</li>
<li>Reuse those URLs</li>
<li>Implementing 301 redirect</li>
</ol>

<h5 id="keep-alive-as-long-as-search-demand-is-high">Keep alive as long as search demand is high</h5>
<p>If, despite a product being permanently discontinued, search demand for that particular product remains high you can keep on your site, and treat it as if it were a <em>temporarily discontinued product</em>. The only exception is that now you'll clearly state that the product won't be available ever again.</p>
<p>This way, visitors will still land on the page they were searching for. They won't be able to buy the product, but you showcase alternatives that get the job done too just as well. This approach leads to more conversions ‚Äî at the end of the day, that's what we're after right?</p>
<p>Then, when the search demand dies out, make the following change to the product page:</p>
<ol>
<li>Remove internal links pointing to it</li>
<li>Remove it from the <a href="/academy/xml-sitemap/">XML Sitemap</a>
</li>
<li>Remove it from on-site search</li>
<li>Apply the <a href="/academy/meta-robots-tag/#noindex">noindex directive</a>
</li>
</ol>
<p>Implement a periodical process in which you'll double-check the products have served their purpose, and remove them completely ‚Äî serving a 410 status code.</p>

<p>An approach we take is "noindexing" by intent, which takes into consideration which type of keywords a product may be ranking for and what users are using within internal search.</p>
<p>For instance, if a graphics card ranks for something very specific to that model, such as <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">NVIDIA 1080Ti</code>, we have to consider the ramifications of what to do if we 301 or 410 this page. 301-ing it to a similar product may confuse the user - they were looking for that specific product and have ended up somewhere else. This means they may abandon the search right there and look at a different site until they find out the product is discontinued and start a new line of research. A similar thing may well happen if we 410 the page because it had low SEO value, if for instance it was bookmarked or got some referral traffic.</p>
<p>In these instances, I would consider using a selective noindex strategy. This means, clearly marking the product as discontinued and offering links to the closest alternatives that fulfil the same job, which has been demonstrably shown to improve conversion rates. <h5 id="reuse-URL">Reuse those URLs</h5>
<p>If the URL of the discontinued product is reusable for other, highly related, products, then you can simply adjust the content and keep the page itself alive. Alternatively, you can use the URL for a new landing page that refers to the previous content, or to a portfolio of alternative relevant products.</p>
<p>If the URL doesn‚Äôt have any such use, but still holds SEO value, the easiest way to utilize it is to implement a <a href="/academy/redirects/faq/301-redirect/">301 redirect</a> to a highly relevant URL. However, this is easier said than done.</p>

<h5 id="implement-301-redirects-to-highly-relevant-url-to-prevent-soft-404-errors">Implement 301 redirects to highly relevant URL to prevent soft 404 errors</h5>
<p>Redirecting your permanently discontinued product pages to products that aren't very relevant can result in errors that you‚Äôd do best to avoid.</p>
<p>Let‚Äôs say you‚Äôre a former retailer of black socks, but for some reason, this very type of socks has disappeared from the Earth‚Äôs surface for good (probably a washing machine ate them all). As these socks were super popular, their product pages have gained a lot of links and traffic. And you want to use that traffic to sell other products.</p>
<p>Using a 301 redirect, you can suggest other‚Äîlarger, smaller, or warmer‚Äîsocks that are highly similar to the black socks of yore. This way, the value of your URL remains and you can still profit from the discontinued version.</p>

<p>Always implement 301 redirects to the most relevant or similar product to ensure you don't experience high bounce rates as people didn't expect to see another product, especially one that's not relevant to the product they were searching for in the first place.</p>
<p>For an even better user experience, add a blurb of text to the redirect's target page stating the product they were looking for is discontinued and how this current product is perhaps relevant to them instead.</p>

<h5 id="irrelevant-redirect">What happens if you redirect to an irrelevant page?</h5>
<p>But if you use the same redirect to link to an irrelevant item‚Äîlet‚Äôs say a gray hoodie‚Äîit might happen that the search engines will evaluate such a redirect as a soft 404 error, meaning that you will lose all the SEO benefits.</p>
<p>The same applies for <a href="/academy/redirects/">redirects</a> to categories, or even your homepage. If you have the opportunity, it pays to redirect items to items and categories to categories to avoid such an unfortunate outcome. Redirecting to homepage should be your last option.</p>

<blockquote class="quote-box__blockquote">
<p>While there is no certainty whether Google is going to treat your redirect as a soft 404 or a 301 for discontinued product pages, it's almost always a good idea to analyze the backlink profile of the discontinued pages first before you decide on an approach. Link reclamation might be a much better option than a 301 redirect if the links are also relevant to another product page. This gets rid of any concerns involving the diminishing link equity of 301 redirects.</p>
<p>All you have to do is find all the links to the discontinued product pages on Search Console or any other backlink checker tool and ask websites to update the link to a product page that is available and relevant.</p>

<h2 id="three-qualities">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#three-qualities" tabindex="-1"></a>Three things to keep in mind</h2>
<p>When dealing with discontinued products, keep in mind that you need to take into consideration these three goals:</p>
<ol>
<li>Preserving crawl budget</li>
<li>Preserving page value</li>
<li>Providing a good user experience</li>
</ol>
<p>There's no ‚Äúone size fits all‚Äù approach to this, so adjust your strategy based on which of the goals above you need to focus on the most.</p>

<h3 class="toc" id="preserve-crawl-budget">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#preserve-crawl-budget" tabindex="-1"></a>Preserving crawl budget</h3>
<p>When it comes to retaining the right balance of crawl budget and link value, we recommend that you monitor internal links leading to 404 or 410 pages.</p>
<h3 class="toc" id="preserve-page-value">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#preserve-page-value" tabindex="-1"></a>Preserving page value</h3>
<p>But don‚Äôt just look at your own site. You also need to periodically check if there are any backlinks returning 4xx errors on your site that need to be redirected.</p>
<h3 class="toc" id="user-experience">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#user-experience" tabindex="-1"></a>Providing a good user experience</h3>
<p>When you‚Äôre deciding on a solution to discontinued product pages, you should always take into account how your visitors will feel about your solution. Let this drive your approach to dealing with these pages. At the end of the day, every web site is different and may require a (slightly) different approach.</p>
<p>What about the situation in which product demand remains high?</p>
<p>Sometimes when a product is discontinued permanently, it remains popular among users and the demand is still high. When this happens, you can utilize its popularity and cater to people who would like to know more about it.</p>
<p>A popular beauty feature from Urban Decay called Naked Palette can serve as a good example here. This 12-shadow eyeliner set was a must for many women until August 2018, when the company discontinued its production.</p>
<p>The product has retained its popularity to this day and has bounced back a few times, as you can see on Google Trends.</p>

<p>Apart from displaying alternative products, the discontinued product page can serve as a reminder of a once legendary item and can provide information to customers. And informational content has huge value for overall SEO performance.</p>

<p>Displaying a 404 or empty page to your beloved customers will not only result in a bad user experience but also in lost sales. When this happens at scale, this can have a significant impact in revenue for your site (and not only for organic, but for other channels too).</p>

<h2 id="closing-thoughts">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#closing-thoughts" tabindex="-1"></a>Closing thoughts</h2>
<p>Dealing with discontinued products is all about balancing preservation of crawl budget and page value, while still satisfying the users that visit your site.</p>
<p>There is no ‚Äúone size fits all‚Äù approach to dealing with discontinued product pages, but there‚Äôs one thing we know for sure: your visitors are the only ones who‚Äôll purchase products on your site. A satisfying user experience should be topmost, as that‚Äôs what‚Äôs going to make you money.</p>
<h2 id="faq-discontinued">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#faq-discontinued" tabindex="-1"></a>Frequently asked questions about discontinued products</h2>
<h3 id="what-is-discontinued-product">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#what-is-discontinued-product" tabindex="-1"></a>‚èπÔ∏è What is a discontinued product?</h3>
<p>A discontinued product is a product that has become either temporarily or permanently unavailable for purchase.</p>
<h3 id="should-i-keep-discontinued-product-pages-up">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#should-i-keep-discontinued-product-pages-up" tabindex="-1"></a>üë©‚Äçüíª Should I keep discontinued product pages up?</h3>
<p>Whether or not to keep a discontinued product‚Äôs page alive depends on whether the product is discontinued temporarily or permanently, as well as whether it possesses SEO value.</p>
<h3 id="how-do-i-preserve-SEO-">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#how-do-i-preserve-SEO-" tabindex="-1"></a>üìà How do I preserve the SEO value that product pages have?</h3>
<p>You can reuse the page for other products, landing pages, or portfolios, or you can implement a 301 redirect to a highly relevant product page.</p>
<h3 id="redirect-discontinued-page-to-homepage">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor-link" href="#redirect-discontinued-page-to-homepage" tabindex="-1"></a>‚Ü©Ô∏è Should I redirect discontinued products to the homepage or product category page?</h3>
<p>No, redirecting product pages to categories or your homepage can result in a soft 404 error, meaning you could lose SEO value. Always redirect to a highly relevant segment of your website such as a similar product.</p>
